Gallery — Paintings & Portraits
Napoleon Crossing the Alps
Jacques-Louis David, 1801. Napoleon on a rearing horse at the Great St. Bernard Pass, inscribed with the names of Hannibal and Charlemagne.
The Coronation of Napoleon
Jacques-Louis David, 1807. Napoleon crowns Joséphine at Notre-Dame Cathedral, December 2, 1804, in the presence of Pope Pius VII.
Battle of Austerlitz
François Gérard, 1810. Napoleon's decisive victory over the combined Austrian-Russian army, December 2, 1805 — his greatest triumph.
Bonaparte at the Bridge at Arcole
Antoine-Jean Gros, 1801. Napoleon, 27, seizes a flag and leads his troops across the bridge at Arcole during the Italian Campaign, 1796.
Battle of Waterloo, 1815
The final battle of Napoleon's Hundred Days. Wellington's Anglo-Dutch army held the ridge until Prussian reinforcements under Blücher arrived, ending the Empire.
Napoleon in His Study
Jacques-Louis David, 1812. Napoleon at 4 AM in his study at the Tuileries, having just completed work on the Napoleonic Code — candlelight, uniform, quill.
Marshal Michel Ney
"The Bravest of the Brave." Napoleon's finest battlefield marshal, Ney led cavalry charges at Waterloo and was executed by firing squad after the Restoration.
Duke of Wellington
Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington — the man who stopped Napoleon at Waterloo. "The nearest run thing you ever saw in your life," he said afterward.
Cuirassiers at Austerlitz
Ernest Meissonier. French heavy cavalry — cuirassiers in steel breastplates — a decisive arm of Napoleon's army at Austerlitz and across the Empire's campaigns.
Bivouac on the Eve of Austerlitz
The night of December 1, 1805. Napoleon walked among his troops as they lit torches to honor the anniversary of his coronation — one of the most celebrated scenes of the Grande Armée.
Napoleon Bonaparte
Portrait of the Emperor of the French. Napoleon Bonaparte (1769–1821) — artillery officer, First Consul, Emperor, exile — the most consequential figure of the modern age.
