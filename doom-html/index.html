DOOM — A History
1990
id Software Foundedcompany
John Carmack, John Romero, Adrian Carmack, and Tom Hall leave Softdisk. Wolfenstein 3D ships in 1992 and proves the FPS market — but it's just the warmup act.
1993
Doom Released via Sharewarerelease
Doom 1.0 drops on December 10, 1993 — uploaded to an FTP server at the University of Wisconsin. The shareware model distributes Episode 1 free. FTP servers crash under demand. An estimated 10 million people download it in two months.
1994
Deathmatch & Network Playmilestone
Doom's IPX network multiplayer and deathmatch mode ignite competitive online gaming. Office networks grind to a halt. The term "deathmatch" enters gaming vocabulary permanently.
1994
Doom II: Hell on Earthrelease
Doom II ships retail-only and sells 2 million copies in its first year. It introduces the Super Shotgun, the Icon of Sin final boss, and larger open maps.
1997
Source Code Releasedmilestone
id Software releases the Doom source code under a non-commercial license (later GPL in 1999). Within days ports appear on Linux, BeOS, and dozens of platforms. ZDoom, PrBoom, GZDoom are born.
2004
Doom 3 — Survival Horror Rebootrelease
Doom 3 launches with cutting-edge lighting, oppressive darkness, and scripted scares. It sells 3.5 million copies but divides fans. The id Tech 4 engine is widely licensed.
2016
Doom (2016) — Arena Revivalrelease
Doom 2016 resurrects arena FPS with glory kills, demon speed, and constant forward pressure. Won multiple GOTY awards and is credited with single-handedly reviving the genre.
2020
Doom Eternalrelease
Doom Eternal raises mechanical complexity to its peak — resource management, platforming, and a weapon wheel that demands mastery. Sells over 3 million at launch.
2023
Doom Turns 30milestone
The "It Runs Doom" meme documents the game running on pregnancy tests, ATMs, MIDI keyboards, and a John Deere tractor. The original WAD format is still actively used.
2025
Doom: The Dark Agesrelease
The franchise returns to its roots with medieval hell aesthetics, a shield saw, and ground-based combat. id Tech 8 pushes visual fidelity to new heights.
