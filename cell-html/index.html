The animal cell is the basic unit of life. It contains specialized structures called organelles, each with a specific function — much like organs in the body.

Mitosis — Cell Division

Somatic cells divide by mitosis to produce two genetically identical daughter cells.

Interphase
Interphase — Cell prepares for division

During interphase (G1, S, G2), the cell grows, replicates its DNA, and synthesizes proteins needed for division. Chromatin is loosely packed in the nucleus.

Membrane Transport

How molecules cross the phospholipid bilayer.

Passive Diffusion

Small nonpolar molecules (O₂, CO₂, lipids) move freely through the phospholipid bilayer from high to low concentration. No energy (ATP) required. Rate depends on concentration gradient, temperature, and molecule size.
