Other Ferments

Miso

Japanese fermented soybean paste made with koji (Aspergillus oryzae mold grown on rice, barley, or soybeans). Koji produces enzymes that break down protein and starch into amino acids and sugars — deep umami. White miso (shiro): short fermentation 1–3 months, mild and sweet. Red miso (aka): 1–3+ years, intense and salty. Mixed (awase): blend for balance. Always add to soup after turning off heat to preserve beneficial enzymes.

Koji enzymesMonths to yearsDeep umami

Tempeh

Indonesian fermented soybean cake. Rhizopus oligosporus mold (and related species) grow through cooked, dehulled soybeans in solid-state fermentation. The white mycelium binds beans into a firm, sliceable cake with a mushroom-like, nutty flavor. Ferments at 85–88°F for 24–48 hours. Higher protein digestibility than unfermented soybeans. Phytic acid largely eliminated. Slice and pan-fry for crispy edges or marinate and grill.

Rhizopus mold85–88°F24–48 hours

Sauerkraut

Finely shredded cabbage lacto-fermented with only salt (2% by weight). Massage the cabbage vigorously after salting to break cell walls and release liquid — this becomes the brine. Pack tightly into a jar, press below brine, and ferment 1–4 weeks at cool room temperature (65–75°F). German and Alsatian cuisine cornerstone. Aged sauerkraut (months in a crock) develops profound complexity. Rich in Vitamin C — historically prevented scurvy on ships.

2% salt1–4 weeksCabbage only

Fermented Hot Sauce

Blend chiles with 2–3% salt, ferment 5–14 days in an airlock jar. The lacto-fermentation adds complexity and fruity depth that fresh hot sauces lack — the acidity is rounder and more integrated. After fermentation, blend with vinegar (to taste), strain, bottle. Tabasco is fermented in white oak barrels for up to 3 years. Fermented habanero-mango sauce: intensely fruity with building heat. Keeps for months refrigerated.

2–3% saltAirlock5–14 days

Pickles (Lacto vs Vinegar)

True lacto-fermented pickles: cucumbers in salt brine (3–5%), fermented 3–7 days. Crunchy, complex, probiotic — the original "dill pickle." Requires a tannin source (grape leaf, oak leaf, or horseradish leaf) to keep pickles crisp. Vinegar pickles (quick pickles): cucumber submerged in hot vinegar brine — ready in 24 hours but contain no live cultures and have a simpler flavor. "Vlasic" style is vinegar; traditional Jewish deli pickles are lacto.

Beer & Wine Basics

Beer: malted barley steeped in hot water (mash) to extract fermentable sugars, boiled with hops, cooled, pitched with brewer's yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae for ales, S. pastorianus for lagers), fermented 1–4 weeks. Wine: crushed grapes (natural yeast on skins or added wine yeast) ferment juice 1–4 weeks. Wild/natural wine uses only native yeasts. Both require clean sanitized equipment — the enemy is wild bacteria and mold, not the intended yeast.
