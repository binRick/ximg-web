Kombucha

SCOBY Explained

Symbiotic Culture Of Bacteria and Yeast. A rubbery, cellulose disc produced by Acetobacter bacteria as a protective mat. Not technically required (liquid culture works fine) but traditional. The SCOBY grows larger with each batch; remove the top layers periodically. Store extra SCOBYs in a "SCOBY hotel" — a jar with starter liquid and a loose lid. SCOBYs can be dried, composted, or eaten.

First Fermentation (F1)

Brew sweet tea: 1 gallon water, 1 cup sugar, 4–6 tea bags (black tea is traditional; green tea gives a milder flavor). Cool to room temperature. Add SCOBY and 1–2 cups starter liquid (previous batch or raw apple cider vinegar). Cover with breathable cloth (not airtight). Ferment 7–14 days at 68–78°F. Taste daily after day 7. Done when pleasantly tart. pH should reach 2.5–3.5.

Second Fermentation (F2) — Carbonation

Bottle the F1 liquid in flip-top bottles (Grolsch-style). Add flavorings — ginger, fruit juice, berries, citrus. Seal tightly. Leave at room temperature 2–5 days. The residual yeast ferments residual sugar, producing CO₂ that dissolves into the liquid under pressure. "Burp" bottles daily to check pressure and prevent explosion. Move to fridge when fizzy enough. Cold stops fermentation.

Flavoring Options

Ginger + lemon (classic): 1 tsp fresh ginger juice + squeeze of lemon per 16oz bottle. Mixed berry: 2 tbsp frozen berries per bottle. Elderflower: 2 tbsp elderflower cordial. Hibiscus: 1 tsp dried hibiscus flowers (beautiful magenta color). Mango + habanero: exotic heat. Lavender + blueberry. Passion fruit. The sugar in fruit juice drives F2 carbonation. Avoid: citrus pith (bitter), artificial sweeteners (yeast can't ferment them).

pH Monitoring

Test pH throughout fermentation with cheap pH strips or a digital meter. Starting pH (sweet tea): ~7. After F1: target 2.5–3.5. Below 2.5 = over-fermented, too vinegary. Above 3.5 = under-fermented, too sweet. pH strips accurate to ±0.5 — adequate for home brewing. Calibrated digital meters (±0.01) for those who want precision. A healthy, active F1 batch should reach target pH in 7–10 days at 74°F.

Continuous Brew Method

Use a large vessel (1–2.5 gallon) with a spigot at the bottom. Harvest kombucha from the spigot daily, replacing with fresh sweet tea. The large volume creates a stable pH environment, reducing risk of contamination. Flavor develops more complexity over time. Requires weekly cleaning every 2–3 months. Produces a milder, more consistent kombucha than batch brewing. Popular with daily drinkers.
