Civil War Timeline
Nov 1860
Lincoln Elected
Abraham Lincoln wins the presidency without a single Southern electoral vote. South Carolina secedes within weeks. By February 1861, seven states have formed the Confederacy.
Apr 1861
Fort Sumter — War Begins
Confederate forces bombard Fort Sumter in Charleston Harbor. No one is killed in the bombardment, but the nation is at war. Lincoln calls for 75,000 volunteers; four more states secede.
Sep 1862
Emancipation Proclamation Issued
After Antietam, Lincoln issues the Emancipation Proclamation: as of January 1, 1863, enslaved people in Confederate states are declared free. The war becomes explicitly about slavery — preventing British/French recognition of the Confederacy.
Jul 1863
Gettysburg & Vicksburg
The double turning point. Gettysburg ends Confederate offensive capability in the East; Vicksburg splits the Confederacy and gives the Union control of the Mississippi. The South can no longer win — only fight on.
Nov 1863
Gettysburg Address
Lincoln dedicates the Gettysburg cemetery in 272 words. "Government of the people, by the people, for the people, shall not perish from the earth." Redefines the war's purpose and America's democratic promise.
Apr 1865
Lee Surrenders at Appomattox
After Grant's relentless Overland Campaign, Lee has no option but surrender. The war ends. Lincoln is assassinated on April 14. Reconstruction begins — 13th Amendment abolishes slavery everywhere in the United States.
