The Double Slit Experiment

Fire particles one at a time through two slits. With observation OFF, each particle passes through both slits simultaneously (as a wave) and interferes with itself — creating bright/dark fringes on the screen. With observation ON (detecting which slit), the particle must "choose" a slit, destroying the interference pattern. The act of knowing which path collapses the wave.
