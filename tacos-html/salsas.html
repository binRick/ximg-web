Salsas — The soul of the taco

A taco without salsa is incomplete. Mexican salsa culture is staggering in variety — raw and cooked, smooth and chunky, mild and face-melting. Every taqueria has a house salsa that defines its identity.

Salsa Roja
Red · Cooked
Roasted tomatoes, serranos or jalapeños, garlic, and onion blended smooth. The baseline salsa of Mexico. Heat level varies widely by recipe and region.
Salsa Verde
Green · Tomatillo
Charred or boiled tomatillos with serrano chiles, cilantro, and garlic. Tangy and bright. Pairs especially well with pork tacos and carnitas.
Pico de Gallo
Fresh · Raw
Diced raw tomato, white onion, jalapeño, cilantro, lime juice, salt. No cooking. Maximum freshness — the antithesis of a smooth salsa. Best on fish and carne asada.
Salsa de Habanero
Orange · Nuclear
Roasted habanero with tomato and vinegar. Intensely fruity heat — habaneros are floral and citrusy before the searing pain arrives. Yucatan's essential salsa.
Salsa Negra
Black · Deep
Dried chile negro or mulato blended with garlic and lard. Dark, complex, slightly bitter, almost smoky-sweet. Goes especially well with mushroom or chicken tacos.
Salsa de Árbol
Red · Fiery
Chile de árbol (small, very hot) with toasted sesame seeds, tomato, and vinegar. Thin, sharp, intensely hot. The go-to high-heat salsa at most taquerias.
Guacamole
Avocado · Fresh
Mashed Hass avocado with lime, salt, cilantro, onion, serrano. Simple at its best — resist the urge to add extras. A whole food group in taco culture.
Crema & Lime Sauce
White · Creamy
Mexican crema (thinner than sour cream) with lime zest and sometimes chipotle puree. Drizzled on fish tacos for cooling contrast to the heat.
Mole Negro
Complex · Oaxacan
Over 20 ingredients: dried chiles, chocolate, plantain, spices, herbs. Black as night. A labor of love — some moles take 3 days to make. Poured over shredded turkey or chicken.
Salsa Taquera
Al Pastor Companion
Taquero-style red chile sauce specific to al pastor stands — slightly oily, thick, made from guajillo and morita chiles. Drizzled from a squeeze bottle by the taquero.
