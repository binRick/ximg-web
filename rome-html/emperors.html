Famous Emperors
Augustus (Octavian)
27 BC – 14 AD
Rome's first emperor and arguably its greatest. Ended a century of civil war, established the Principate, reformed the military, built the Praetorian Guard, and launched a golden age of arts under the Pax Romana.
Good Ruler
Julius Caesar
Dictator 49–44 BC
Conquered Gaul (58–50 BC), crossed the Rubicon, reformed the calendar, and centralized power. Assassinated on the Ides of March (15 March 44 BC) by Senate conspirators led by Brutus and Cassius.
Dictator
Trajan
98 – 117 AD
Rome's greatest military emperor. Expanded the Empire to its maximum extent — Dacia, Arabia, Mesopotamia. Built Trajan's Column and Trajan's Market in Rome. Declared Optimus Princeps ("Best Leader").
Optimus Princeps
Hadrian
117 – 138 AD
Philosopher-emperor and builder. Constructed Hadrian's Wall across Britain, rebuilt the Pantheon, and created the Villa Adriana. Consolidated the empire by abandoning Mesopotamia and focusing on defensive frontiers.
Builder
Marcus Aurelius
161 – 180 AD
The Stoic philosopher-emperor. Spent most of his reign on the Danube frontier fighting Germanic tribes. His "Meditations" remains a masterpiece of philosophy. Last of the Five Good Emperors.
Philosopher King
Nero
54 – 68 AD
Infamous for excess and cruelty. Blamed Christians for the Great Fire of Rome (64 AD). Executed his own mother Agrippina. Persecuted the Senate. Committed suicide after being declared a public enemy.
Tyrant
Caligula
37 – 41 AD
Began with promise, descended into megalomania. Declared himself a living god, humiliated senators, and made his horse Incitatus a consul (possibly as an insult to the Senate). Assassinated by the Praetorian Guard.
Mad Emperor
Constantine I
306 – 337 AD
Reunified the empire after civil war. Issued the Edict of Milan (313 AD) legalizing Christianity. Founded Constantinople (330 AD) as the new eastern capital. Presided over the First Council of Nicaea.
Transformer
Diocletian
284 – 305 AD
Stabilized the Crisis of the Third Century. Created the Tetrarchy (rule of four), split the empire into halves, reformed taxation and the military. Launched the last great persecution of Christians.
Reformer
