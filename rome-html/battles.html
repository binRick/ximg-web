Famous Battles
Battle of Cannae
2 August 216 BC
Hannibal Barca's Carthaginian army encircles and destroys a Roman force of ~86,000 — one of the worst defeats in Roman history. An estimated 47,000–70,000 Romans are killed in a single day. Taught Rome the art of the double envelopment.
Outcome: Roman defeat; Hannibal fails to capitalize; Rome rebuilds and fights on
Battle of Zama
19 October 202 BC
Scipio Africanus defeats Hannibal in North Africa, ending the Second Punic War. Rome's cavalry under Masinissa turns the battle by returning to strike Hannibal's rear. Scipio reverses the very tactics Hannibal used at Cannae.
Outcome: Roman victory; Carthage stripped of territory and fleet; Scipio earns "Africanus"
Battle of Alesia
52 BC
Caesar besieges the Gallic stronghold of Alesia, building 18 km of fortifications around it and another 21 km facing outward against a relief army. Vercingetorix surrenders. The engineering feat is considered one of history's greatest sieges.
Outcome: Decisive Roman victory; Gaul conquered; Caesar's power secured
Battle of Actium
2 September 31 BC
Octavian's fleet under Agrippa defeats Mark Antony and Cleopatra's combined force off the Greek coast. Antony and Cleopatra flee to Egypt where they later commit suicide. Ends the last Roman civil war.
Outcome: Octavian becomes sole ruler; Egyptian kingdom absorbed; Roman Empire begins
Battle of the Teutoburg Forest
9 AD
Germanic chieftain Arminius (Hermann) lures three Roman legions (XVII, XVIII, XIX) into an ambush in the forests of Germania. ~20,000 Romans are killed. Augustus reportedly cried "Varus, give me back my legions!"
Outcome: Roman defeat; Rhine becomes permanent northern frontier; three lost legions never replaced
Siege of Jerusalem
70 AD
Titus besieges and destroys Jerusalem during the First Jewish-Roman War. The Second Temple is burned. Over a million civilians are killed or die of famine and disease. ~97,000 are enslaved. The Arch of Titus in Rome commemorates the victory.
Outcome: Jewish diaspora; Jerusalem razed; massive Roman triumph celebrated
