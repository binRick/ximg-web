🌱 Spring ☀️ Summer 🍂 Fall ❄️ Winter 📅 Year-Round Market Tips Storage

Summer — June through August

Tomatoes
Peak: July–September
Heirloom varieties: Brandywine, Cherokee Purple, Green Zebra. Smell the stem end — strong tomato fragrance = ripe. Never refrigerate.
Corn
Peak: July–August
Sugars convert to starch quickly after harvest. Buy same day, cook same day. Peel back husk to check plump, firm kernels.
Zucchini & Summer Squash
Peak: June–August
Pick small (6–8 inches) for better flavor and fewer seeds. Farmers markets often have unusual varieties — pattypan, eight-ball, costata romanesco.
Peppers
Peak: July–September
Bell, shishito, padron, Hungarian wax, Jimmy Nardello (sweet frying pepper). Color indicates ripeness — red is fully ripe, sweetest.
Eggplant
Peak: July–September
Should feel heavy, skin taut and glossy. Globe, Japanese, Italian, Chinese varieties. Japanese and Chinese have fewer seeds, less bitterness.
Peaches & Nectarines
Peak: July–August
Freestone vs clingstone. Smell near the stem — fragrant means ripe. Slight give when pressed at the shoulders. Refrigerate only when fully ripe.
Blueberries
Peak: July–August
Should have a bloom (silvery coating) — sign of freshness. Highbush vs lowbush (wild — more intense flavor). Refrigerate immediately.
Watermelon
Peak: July–August
Look for a creamy yellow field spot (where it sat on ground). Sounds hollow when tapped. Heavier = juicier. Ask farmers when it was picked.
Basil
Peak: June–September
Don't refrigerate — turns black. Store in a glass of water at room temp. Genovese, Thai, lemon, purple — all peak in summer.
