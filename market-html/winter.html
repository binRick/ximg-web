🌱 Spring ☀️ Summer 🍂 Fall ❄️ Winter 📅 Year-Round Market Tips Storage

Winter — December through February

Blood Oranges
Peak: January–March
Moro, Tarocco, Sanguinello varieties. Anthocyanins develop from cold nights — more color = colder growing conditions. Raspberry-berry notes.
Grapefruit
Peak: December–April
Ruby Red, Oro Blanco, Star Ruby. Heavy for size = juicy. Florida vs Texas vs California have different flavor profiles. Pairs well with salt and honey.
Meyer Lemons
Peak: December–March
Lemon × mandarin cross. Sweeter, less acidic, floral. Thin skin. Use zest and juice interchangeably with regular lemon but expect milder acidity.
Kale & Lacinato Kale
Peak: November–March
Frost-sweetened. Lacinato (dinosaur/cavolo nero) has more tender leaves. Massage raw kale with oil to soften. Pairs with lemon and garlic.
Celeriac / Celery Root
Peak: November–March
Knobby root. Nutty, celery-like flavor. Remoulade (shredded raw with mustard mayo) is the classic French preparation. Roasts beautifully.
Sunchoke / Jerusalem Artichoke
Peak: November–February
Nutty, earthy, slightly sweet. Unrelated to artichokes. Inulin content can cause digestive discomfort ("fartichoke"). Roast or make into soup.
Fennel
Peak: October–March
Anise-licorice flavor that mellows when cooked. Fronds are a garnish. Shave raw into salads or roast/braise until caramelized and tender.
Chestnuts
Peak: October–December
Score the flat side before roasting (prevents explosion). Fresh chestnuts store in the fridge. Starchy and sweet — roast, braise, or make chestnut soup.
