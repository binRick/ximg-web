🌱 Spring ☀️ Summer 🍂 Fall ❄️ Winter 📅 Year-Round Market Tips Storage

How to Store Market Finds

ItemLocationDurationNotes
TomatoesCounter3–5 daysNever refrigerate — cold destroys texture and flavor. Keep at room temp away from sunlight.
Peaches, NectarinesCounter → Fridge1–2 days counter, 5 days fridgeRipen at room temp; refrigerate only when ripe. Eat same day as refrigerating when possible.
BerriesFridge3–5 daysDon't wash until use. Store dry. Line container with paper towel to absorb moisture.
Leafy GreensFridge5–7 daysHigh humidity drawer. Wrap loosely in damp paper towel inside a bag. Wilted greens can be revived in ice water.
Root VegetablesFridge2–4 weeksRemove greens — they draw moisture from roots. Carrots, beets, turnips keep well refrigerated.
Herbs (soft)Counter1 weekBasil, cilantro, parsley: trim stems, store in glass of water at room temp. Cover loosely with bag.
Herbs (hard)Fridge2–3 weeksRosemary, thyme, sage: wrap in damp paper towel, store in bag in fridge.
Winter SquashCool, dark2–6 monthsStore whole at 50–55°F. Do NOT refrigerate uncut squash. Once cut, refrigerate and use within a week.
Garlic & OnionsCool, dark, dry1–3 monthsNeed airflow — not sealed containers. Keep apart from each other and potatoes.
MushroomsFridge5–7 daysPaper bag absorbs excess moisture. Never plastic bags. Wipe clean, don't rinse. Slightly open bag for airflow.
Ethylene ProducersIsolated—Apples, pears, bananas produce ethylene gas that accelerates ripening. Store away from sensitive produce (leafy greens, broccoli, carrots).
