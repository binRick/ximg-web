Noodles — Matching noodle to broth

Ramen noodles are made from wheat flour, water, and kansui — an alkaline mineral water that gives ramen noodles their characteristic yellow color and springy texture. The thickness, waviness, and density of the noodle must be matched to the broth's viscosity.

Thin Straight Noodles
細ストレート麺
Standard for Hakata tonkotsu. The thin noodle surfaces let the rich broth cling tightly. Cooks in 60–90 seconds. Hakata shops offer kaedama — a refill of noodles into your remaining broth.
Wavy Medium Noodles
縮れ麺 (中)
The most versatile ramen noodle. Wavy texture traps broth in the folds. Standard for Tokyo shoyu, many regional styles. Coils in the bowl beautifully. Perfect medium between delicate and hearty.
Thick Wavy Noodles
太縮れ麺
Classic Sapporo miso noodle. Wide, heavily wavy, substantial chew. Matches the assertive, rich miso broth. Takes 3–4 minutes to cook. Stays al dente longer without going mushy.
Flat Wide Noodles
平打ち麺
Kitakata-style — unusually flat and wide, hand-cut appearance. Chewy, rustic texture. Pairs with a light shoyu broth. Visually distinctive; more similar to pasta than typical ramen noodles.
Curly Noodles
縮れ麺 (強)
Tightly curled, springy. Common in tsukemen (dipping ramen) where the noodle must hold its texture out of the broth. The curls create pockets that hold dipping sauce.
Egg Noodles (Chukamen)
中華麺
The original ramen noodle with a higher egg content — richer yellow color, more tender bite. The standard Chinese-style noodle adapted for Japanese ramen. Medium thickness.
