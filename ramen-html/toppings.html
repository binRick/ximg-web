Toppings — The art of the bowl

A ramen bowl is composed like a painting. Each topping contributes a different texture, flavor, or visual element. The combination of toppings signals the regional style as much as the broth does.

Chashu Pork
チャーシュー
Pork belly or shoulder rolled tight, tied, then braised or slow-roasted in soy, mirin, sake, and sugar. Sliced into tender rounds. The crown jewel of most ramen bowls.
Soft-Boiled Egg
味玉 · Ajitsuke Tamago
Egg boiled exactly 6–7 minutes, peeled, then marinated in soy sauce, mirin, and sake. Jammy, custard-like yolk with savory-sweet exterior. The most anticipated bite in the bowl.
Nori
海苔
Sheet of dried seaweed standing upright in the broth. As it softens, it absorbs the broth and becomes a delicious, umami-packed leaf. Visually iconic in the bowl.
Menma
メンマ · Bamboo Shoots
Fermented bamboo shoots — crunchy, slightly sour, soy-marinated. A classic ramen topping that provides textural contrast against the tender noodles and pork.
Green Onion
ネギ
Thinly sliced scallion sprinkled on the surface. Fresh, pungent, cutting through the richness of fatty broths. Some shops have a dedicated green onion station to customize.
Corn
コーン
Kernels of sweet corn — a Sapporo miso ramen signature. Adds bursts of sweetness against the savory miso base. Often added with a knob of butter that melts into the broth.
Butter
バター
A cube of butter placed on Hokkaido ramen — melts into the broth, enriching it further and adding a sweet dairy roundness. Divisive but deeply satisfying in cold-weather ramen.
Narutomaki
なると巻き
White fish cake with a pink spiral design — the classic ramen topping from illustrations. Named after the Naruto whirlpools. More decorative than flavorful; chewy, mild, nostalgic.
Bean Sprouts
もやし
Mung bean sprouts — crispy, fresh, adds lightness to rich broths. Standard in miso and tonkotsu bowls. Often stir-fried briefly in sesame oil before adding.
Black Garlic Oil
黒マー油
Charred garlic blended with sesame oil — swirled on tonkotsu ramen to add smoky, almost burnt garlic depth. The defining flavor note of Kumamoto-style ramen.
Tofu
豆腐
Silken or firm tofu cubes. Common in vegetarian ramen and some miso styles. Absorbs broth flavor. A cooling, protein-rich addition.
Sesame Seeds
ごま
Toasted white or black sesame sprinkled on top. Nutty aroma, slight crunch. Especially prominent in tantanmen (spicy sesame ramen) where sesame is a structural flavor.
Kikurage Mushroom
キクラゲ
Wood ear mushrooms — sliced thin, slippery, slightly crunchy. Classic tonkotsu topping. Almost tasteless alone but carries broth flavor beautifully.
Spinach or Bok Choy
青菜
Blanched greens placed neatly beside other toppings. Adds color, mild bitterness, and lightness to otherwise rich bowls. Standard in shoyu and shio styles.
