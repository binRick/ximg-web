Queen Victoria, 1882
Formal portrait by Alexander Bassano. Victoria reigned 1837–1901, the longest-reigning British monarch until Elizabeth II, presiding over the Empire's greatest expansion.
The British Empire at its Peak, 1921
Territories shown in red — 13.7 million square miles, covering nearly a quarter of the Earth's land surface. "The sun never sets on the British Empire."
Battle of Trafalgar, 1805
Painting by Clarkson Stanfield. Nelson's decisive victory over the French and Spanish fleets cemented British naval supremacy for a full century and made global empire possible.
HMS Victory — Nelson's Flagship
The 104-gun first-rate ship of the line. Still afloat in Portsmouth, HMS Victory is the world's oldest naval vessel still in commission, a symbol of British naval supremacy.
East India House, London, 1817
Headquarters of the East India Company, the world's first multinational corporation. From this building, a private trading company administered an empire of 200 million people for over two centuries.
Duke of Wellington
Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington, victor at Waterloo (1815). His defeat of Napoleon ended the Napoleonic Wars and launched the Pax Britannica — a century of British global dominance.
Winston Churchill, c. 1941
The last unapologetic defender of Empire. Churchill saved democracy from Nazi Germany while simultaneously fighting to preserve British colonial rule — a contradition that defined his legacy.
