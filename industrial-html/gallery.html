James Watt's Steam Engine
The original Boulton & Watt rotative engine, c. 1788 — the most transformative machine of the Industrial Revolution.
The Spinning Jenny
James Hargreaves's 1764 multi-spindle spinning frame, which multiplied thread production and launched the textile revolution.
Iron Bridge, Coalbrookdale
The world's first cast iron bridge (1779) over the River Severn at Coalbrookdale — a monument to industrial engineering.
Power Loom in Action, 1835
Steam-powered looms in a textile mill, c. 1835 — the factory system that replaced cottage industry and transformed the working class.
James Watt (1736–1819)
Portrait by Carl Frederik von Breda, 1792. Watt's improvements to the steam engine earned him immortality — and the SI unit of power.
Isambard Kingdom Brunel
Robert Howlett's 1857 photograph of Brunel before the launching chains of the SS Great Eastern — the greatest engineer of his age.
Boulton & Watt Engine, 1784
Engineering engraving of the Boulton & Watt rotative steam engine, 1784 — the blueprint that powered the Industrial Revolution.
Watt Steam Pumping Engine
A preserved Watt beam engine — these giants pumped water from mines, enabling the coal and metal extraction that fueled industrialization.
