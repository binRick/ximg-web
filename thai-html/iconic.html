Iconic Thai Dishes — The essential list

Pad Thai
ผัดไทย
Stir-Fried Noodles
Rice noodles stir-fried with egg, tofu or shrimp, bean sprouts, green onions, in a tamarind-fish sauce-palm sugar sauce. Finished with crushed peanuts and lime. Thailand's most famous export — created/promoted as a national dish in the 1940s.
Green Curry
แกงเขียวหวาน
Curry · Coconut
Green curry paste (fresh green chiles, lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime, basil) in coconut milk with chicken, eggplant, and Thai basil. The color comes from fresh green chiles. Fragrant, spicy, and sweet — perhaps the most aromatic Thai curry.
Tom Yum
ต้มยำ
Hot & Sour Soup
Thailand's signature soup — lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, fish sauce, lime juice, chili in a clear or milky broth. Tom yum kung (shrimp) is the classic. Intensely aromatic, simultaneously sour, spicy, and salty.
Som Tum
ส้มตำ
Papaya Salad
Shredded green papaya pounded in a mortar with lime, fish sauce, palm sugar, chili, garlic, tomatoes, and dried shrimp. Isaan origin. The pounding releases the papaya's cells for better sauce absorption. Can contain fermented pla ra (funk level: extreme).
Massaman Curry
แกงมัสมั่น
Mild · Complex
Thailand's most complex curry — influenced by Persian and Malay traders. Dried spices (cardamom, cinnamon, star anise, nutmeg) in coconut milk with potatoes, peanuts, and beef. Mild heat, deeply aromatic. Named best dish in the world by CNN Travel in 2011.
Pad See Ew
ผัดซีอิ๊ว
Wide Noodles
Wide flat rice noodles (sen yai) stir-fried over very high heat with soy sauce, oyster sauce, egg, Chinese broccoli, and chicken or beef. Wok hei (breath of the wok) is essential — the charred smokiness of a ripping-hot wok.
Larb
ลาบ
Minced Meat Salad
Minced chicken, pork, beef, or duck dressed with fish sauce, lime, chili, and toasted rice powder (khao khua) — ground toasted rice that adds nutty flavor and texture. Served with raw herbs, cabbage, and sticky rice. Isaan origin, now national.
Khao Man Gai
ข้าวมันไก่
Poached Chicken Rice
Thai-style Hainanese chicken rice — poached chicken served on rice cooked in the chicken broth, with sliced cucumber, a clear soup, and a pungent ginger-garlic-soybean dipping sauce. The sauce is the differentiator. Comfort food for millions of Thais daily.
Mango Sticky Rice
ข้าวเหนียวมะม่วง
Dessert
Glutinous sticky rice steamed and sweetened with coconut milk and sugar, served with ripe Thai mango and a drizzle of salted coconut cream. Seasonal (April–June when mangoes peak). The best version uses Nam Dok Mai mango.
Tom Kha Gai
ต้มข่าไก่
Coconut Galangal Soup
Chicken soup in coconut milk with galangal (kha), lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, mushrooms, and fish sauce. Milder than tom yum but deeply aromatic. The galangal is the defining flavor — earthier, pinier, less sharp than ginger.
