Thai Noodle Dishes

Thailand's noodle culture was significantly influenced by Chinese immigrants from the 19th century onward. The noodle types — rice, glass, egg — and the cooking methods (stir-fried, braised in broth, cold dressed) span the full range of Thai cuisine's Chinese and indigenous influences.

Pad Thai
ผัดไทย
Rice Noodles · Stir-Fried
Thin rice noodles (sen lek) stir-fried with tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, egg, bean sprouts, and your choice of protein. Finished at the table with lime, peanuts, chili flakes, and fish sauce — adjust to taste. Deceptively complex when made with fresh tamarind.
Pad See Ew
ผัดซีอิ๊ว
Wide Rice Noodles
Flat wide rice noodles (sen yai) in dark soy and oyster sauce, with egg and Chinese broccoli (gai lan). Must be cooked in a screaming-hot wok for proper char. Slightly sweet-savory, smoky. One of the most satisfying stir-fried noodle dishes in Thai cuisine.
Pad Woon Sen
ผัดวุ้นเส้น
Glass Noodles
Cellophane (mung bean starch) noodles stir-fried with egg, vegetables, oyster sauce, and soy sauce. The glass noodles absorb the sauce completely, becoming slippery and intensely flavored. Often made with minced pork, crab, or shrimp.
Khao Soi
ข้าวซอย
Egg Noodles · Curry Broth
Northern specialty: egg noodles in a coconut-curry broth. Two textures — soft braised noodles below and crispy fried noodles on top. The contrast of soft/crispy with the rich broth is the genius of the dish. Unavoidable in Chiang Mai.
Boat Noodles
ก๋วยเตี๋ยวเรือ
Dark Broth · Small Bowls
Small bowls of thin noodles in an intensely dark, spiced broth made with pork or beef blood, five-spice, cinnamon, and star anise. Originally sold from boats in Bangkok's canals. The broth is deeply savory and slightly sweet — ordered by the bowlful, stacked up.
Rad Na
ราดหน้า
Wide Noodles · Gravy
Wide rice noodles topped with a thick, gelatinous oyster sauce-based gravy with Chinese broccoli and pork or seafood. The thick sauce coats the noodles completely. Different from pad see ew — rad na is braised, not stir-fried at high heat.
