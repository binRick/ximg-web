The Solar System

Our solar system formed 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing molecular cloud. Eight planets, hundreds of moons, millions of asteroids, and trillions of comets orbit our star. Each world is unique, hostile, and extraordinary.

Historic Space Missions

From the first steps on the Moon to the deepest look at the universe's infancy, these missions define humanity's greatest achievements in exploration.

Apollo 11
NASA · 1969 · Crewed Lunar Landing
First humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin landed in the Sea of Tranquility on July 20, 1969. Armstrong's "one small step" speech is the most famous moment in space history. 21.5 hours on the surface, 21 kg of lunar samples returned.
Voyager 1
NASA · 1977 · Interstellar Probe
Launched September 5, 1977. Flew past Jupiter (1979) and Saturn (1980). In 2012, became the first human-made object to enter interstellar space. Still transmitting from 24 billion km away, powered by plutonium RTGs.
Hubble Space Telescope
NASA/ESA · 1990 · Space Observatory
Deployed April 24, 1990. Initial mirror flaw corrected in 1993 servicing mission. Has made over 1.4 million observations. Helped determine the age of the universe (13.8 billion years) and confirmed dark energy's existence.
Mars Pathfinder
NASA · 1997 · Mars Lander + Rover
First Mars rover: Sojourner. Demonstrated airbag landing system. Operated 83 days instead of planned 7. Proved that low-cost "faster, better, cheaper" missions could work. Analyzed 16 rock/soil samples.
International Space Station
Multi-agency · 1998–present · Orbital Station
First module (Zarya) launched November 20, 1998. Continuously crewed since November 2, 2000. 109m wide — larger than a football field. 3,600+ days of science experiments. Visible to naked eye from Earth.
Cassini–Huygens
NASA/ESA · 1997–2017 · Saturn Orbiter
Studied Saturn for 13 years. Huygens probe landed on Titan (January 2005) — farthest landing from Earth ever. Discovered active plumes on Enceladus containing water and organics. Grand Finale dive through Saturn's atmosphere in 2017.
Mars Science Laboratory (Curiosity)
NASA · 2011–present · Mars Rover
SUV-sized rover, sky-crane landing system. Confirmed Mars once had conditions suitable for microbial life. Detected organic molecules. Climbed Mount Sharp. Over 4,000 sols (Martian days) of operation as of 2024.
New Horizons
NASA · 2006 · Kuiper Belt Probe
First spacecraft to visit Pluto (July 14, 2015). Revealed Pluto's nitrogen glaciers, towering ice mountains, and surprisingly complex geology. Then flew by Arrokoth (2019) — farthest solar system object ever visited.
James Webb Space Telescope
NASA/ESA/CSA · 2021–present · Space Observatory
Launched December 25, 2021. First full-color images July 2022. Observes in infrared. Can see galaxies from 13.6 billion years ago. Detected water vapor in exoplanet atmospheres. Revealed structure in the very early universe.
Artemis I
NASA · 2022 · Uncrewed Lunar Flyby
First integrated test of SLS/Orion. Launched November 16, 2022. Achieved distant retrograde lunar orbit. Orion traveled 434,522 km from Earth — farthest any spacecraft designed for humans has ever traveled. 25.5-day mission.
Mars Perseverance
NASA · 2020–present · Mars Rover
Landed Jezero Crater February 18, 2021. Carried Ingenuity helicopter — first powered flight on another planet. Caching Martian rock cores for future Earth return. MOXIE experiment produced oxygen from Mars atmosphere.
Parker Solar Probe
NASA · 2018–present · Solar Probe
Fastest human-made object ever built. Closest approach to the Sun: 6.1 million km. Traveling at 690,000 km/h. Entered the Sun's corona in 2021 — first spacecraft to fly through the solar atmosphere. Solving the coronal heating mystery.

Beyond the Solar System

The universe extends 46 billion light-years in every direction. Beyond our solar system lie exoplanets that might harbor life, black holes that warp spacetime, and nebulae where new stars are born.

Exoplanets

Exoplanet — Habitable Zone
Kepler-452b
Distance: 1,400 light-years. Orbits a G-type star 1.5 billion years older than our Sun. 60% larger than Earth (super-Earth). Year = 385 Earth days. Called "Earth's older cousin." Discovered 2015 by the Kepler space telescope.
Exoplanet — Nearest
Proxima Centauri b
Distance: 4.24 light-years — closest known exoplanet to Earth. Orbits Proxima Centauri in 11.2 days. Receives 65% of Earth's stellar flux. Possibly rocky. Tidally locked (one face always toward star). Subject to intense stellar flares.
Exoplanet System — TRAPPIST-1
TRAPPIST-1 System
39 light-years away. 7 Earth-sized planets, 3 in habitable zone (e, f, g). Ultra-cool red dwarf star. Planets are so close together that neighboring worlds would appear larger than our Moon. Announced Feb 2017 — most Earth-sized planets ever found in a single system.
Exoplanet — Hot Jupiter
HD 209458b "Osiris"
150 light-years away. First exoplanet detected transiting its star (1999). First exoplanet with confirmed atmosphere. Atmosphere contains water vapor, oxygen, carbon. Outer atmosphere is being blown away by stellar radiation — a comet-like tail of gas.

Black Holes

Supermassive Black Hole — Milky Way
Sagittarius A*
Mass: 4.1 million solar masses. Distance: 26,000 light-years from Earth. First imaged by Event Horizon Telescope in May 2022. Stars orbit it at thousands of km/s. Event horizon diameter: 25 million km. Relatively quiet — not currently accreting much material.
Supermassive Black Hole — M87 Galaxy
M87* (Pōwehi)
Mass: 6.5 billion solar masses. Distance: 55 million light-years. First black hole ever imaged (April 2019, Event Horizon Telescope). Jets of plasma extend 5,000 light-years from its core. Shadow diameter is larger than our entire solar system.

Nebulae — Stellar Nurseries

Emission Nebula
Orion Nebula (M42)
Distance: 1,344 light-years. 24 light-years across. One of the most studied objects in the sky — visible to naked eye. Active stellar nursery: 700+ young stars forming. Contains the Trapezium Cluster — four hot blue stars at the core.
Emission Nebula
Eagle Nebula (M16)
Distance: 7,000 light-years. Famous for the "Pillars of Creation" — elephant-trunk columns of gas where new stars are forming. Pillars are 5 light-years tall. Hubble photographed them in 1995; JWST re-imaged in extraordinary detail in 2022.
Supernova Remnant
Crab Nebula (M1)
Distance: 6,500 light-years. Remains of a supernova observed by Chinese and Arab astronomers in 1054 AD. At its center: a pulsar spinning 30 times per second. Still expanding at 1,500 km/s. A textbook example of stellar death and recycling.
Planetary Nebula
Ring Nebula (M57)
Distance: 2,283 light-years. A glowing shell of ionized gas expelled by a dying star. The central white dwarf is 200 times hotter than the Sun. Rings are about 1 light-year across. A preview of our own Sun's fate in 5 billion years.

Space Exploration Timeline

Sixty-five years of human expansion into the cosmos, from the first beeping satellite to the most powerful space telescope ever built.

1957
Sputnik 1 — The Space Age Begins
Soviet Union launches the first artificial satellite, October 4, 1957. Its radio beeps shocked the Western world. The Space Race begins immediately.
1961
Yuri Gagarin — First Human in Space
Vostok 1 completes one orbit of Earth on April 12, 1961. Gagarin spends 108 minutes in space. "Poyekhali!" — "Let's go!" — becomes the most famous Russian word.
1965
First Spacewalk
Soviet cosmonaut Alexei Leonov exits Voskhod 2 on March 18, 1965 for 12 minutes. His spacesuit inflates too much; he nearly cannot re-enter. Americans Ed White follows 3 months later.
1969
Apollo 11 — Moon Landing
July 20, 1969: 600 million people watch Armstrong step onto the Moon. "One small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." The cold war's greatest symbolic victory for the United States.
1977
Voyager Program Launches
Voyager 2 (August 20) and Voyager 1 (September 5) launched on a "Grand Tour" of the outer solar system. Both carry the Golden Record — a message to any interstellar civilization that might find them.
1990
Hubble Space Telescope
Deployed from Space Shuttle Discovery. After the mirror flaw repair in 1993, Hubble transformed every field of astronomy — from the age of the universe to deep field images showing thousands of galaxies.
1998
ISS Construction Begins
Module Zarya launched November 20. Represents unprecedented international cooperation between 15 nations. The largest structure ever assembled in space. Continuously inhabited since November 2000.
2012
Voyager 1 Reaches Interstellar Space
On August 25, 2012, Voyager 1 crosses the heliopause — the boundary where the Sun's solar wind yields to interstellar medium. Humanity's first object in interstellar space. 35 years after launch.
2019
First Black Hole Image
Event Horizon Telescope collaboration releases first-ever image of a black hole (M87*) on April 10, 2019. Eight radio telescopes across Earth combined into one Earth-sized virtual telescope. A triumph of global scientific cooperation.
2022
James Webb Space Telescope — First Images
July 12, 2022: NASA releases JWST's first full-color science images. A deep field showing galaxies from 13.1 billion years ago. Stephan's Quintet galaxy cluster. A star-forming region (NGC 3324) in infrared. The clearest view of the cosmos ever achieved.
