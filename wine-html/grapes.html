Grape Varieties

Grenache / Garnacha

World's most widely planted red grape. High alcohol, low tannin, medium acid. Red fruit (strawberry, raspberry, dried cherry), white pepper, herbs. The foundation of Châteauneuf-du-Pape, Priorat (Spain), and Sardinian Cannonau. Old-vine Grenache from Priorat or Gigondas rivals Burgundy in complexity. Blends beautifully in the GSM template.

High alcoholLow tanninPriorat / CdP

Barbera

Piedmont's everyday red — Barbera d'Asti and Barbera d'Alba. Very high acidity (distinctive), low tannins, deep color, dark fruit. Traditionally early-drinking and rustic; modern producers use oak aging for added complexity. Never commands the price of Barolo but delivers genuine pleasure. The Piemontese's daily table wine. Pairs with everything from pizza to braised meats.

Very high acidityLow tanninsPiedmont daily wine

Chenin Blanc

The Loire Valley's great white grape. Extraordinary range: Vouvray (dry to sweet), Savennières (bone-dry, mineral masterpiece), Quarts de Chaume (sweet, noble rot). High acidity and waxy texture are the signatures. South Africa's "Steen" is Chenin Blanc — produces some of the world's best value whites. Ages magnificently — 30+ year Vouvray can be transcendent.

High acidityLoire / South AfricaDry to sweet

Mourvèdre / Monastrell

Dark, tannic, savory variety. Bandol (Provence) is its finest expression — aged in oak, black fruit, olive, game, smoked meat, iron. Needs warmth to ripen fully. Third component in GSM blends, adding structure and spice. Spain calls it Monastrell — concentrated, rustic wines from Jumilla and Yecla. Unusual iron and blood-like mineral note is unmistakable.

Dark fruitSavory / gameyBandol

Muscat

Ancient grape family — arguably the oldest cultivated grape. The most "grapey" of all varieties. Moscato d'Asti (Italy): light, sweet, low-alcohol, effervescent. Muscat de Beaumes-de-Venise: fortified sweet wine, lush and floral. Alsatian Muscat: dry, aromatic. Rutherglen Muscat (Australia): intensely sweet, fortified — raisined, toffee, orange blossom — the world's finest sticky.

Vermentino

Mediterranean white grape thriving in Sardinia, Corsica, and Liguria. Medium body, refreshing acidity, almond bitterness on the finish, citrus, white peach, and saline mineral notes. The saline quality reflects its coastal origins. Vermentino di Gallura DOCG is the top appellation. Excellent everyday wine for seafood and Mediterranean food. Related to Rolle (Provence) and Favorita (Piedmont).
