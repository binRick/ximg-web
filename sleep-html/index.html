Sleep Stage Overview

Wake (W)

Beta waves, full consciousness. Brief awakenings are normal between cycles.

N1 (Light)

1–5 min. Theta waves. Easy to wake. Hypnic jerks common here.

N2 (Light)

Sleep spindles and K-complexes. Memory consolidation begins. 45–55% of total sleep.

N3 (Deep)

Slow-wave sleep. Growth hormone released. Physical repair. Most N3 in first half of night.

REM

Brain active as waking. Vivid dreams. Memory consolidation. Most REM in second half.

24-Hour Circadian Clock

Cortisol

Peaks 30–45 min after waking (cortisol awakening response). Avoid caffeine for first 90 minutes — let cortisol do its job naturally. Chronically high = bad; morning peak = essential.

Melatonin

Secreted by pineal gland 2 hours before sleep. Blue light (screens) suppresses it. Starts rising at ~9PM if you wake at 7AM. Peaks during middle of sleep.

Sleep Debt is Cumulative

You cannot "catch up" on sleep by sleeping in on weekends — this disrupts circadian rhythm. Instead, recover gradually: sleep 1 extra hour per night until debt is cleared. Chronic sleep restriction causes measurable cognitive impairment even if you feel fine.

REM Sleep & Brain Activity

What Happens in REM

  • Eyes move rapidly (REM = Rapid Eye Movement)
  • Brain activity similar to waking
  • Body paralyzed (atonia) — can't act out dreams
  • Vivid, narrative dreams
  • Emotional memory consolidation
  • Creative problem solving

REM Deprivation Effects

  • Emotional dysregulation (irritability)
  • Impaired face reading (can't detect threat)
  • Memory and learning deficits
  • REM rebound on recovery night
  • Associated with depression
