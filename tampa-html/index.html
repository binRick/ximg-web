Old West Tampa

Tampa's Greatest Neighborhood — Bar None

An independent city before Tampa even thought to ask. Built by immigrants who had nothing and created everything. A neighborhood of 120-year-old homes on oak-shaded streets where the coffee is Cuban, the people are real, and the history runs so deep it seeps through the sidewalks. There is no better place to live in Florida. There never has been.

The Greatest Neighborhood in Tampa — and It's Barely Even a Contest
Old West Tampa has everything every other neighborhood is trying to become: authentic history going back 130 years, architecture that can't be replicated, a cultural identity forged by Cuban cigar workers, Spanish mutual-aid societies, and Italian fishermen — and it's sitting five minutes from downtown with room to grow. Every city in America has a neighborhood that insiders know is the real soul of the place. Old West Tampa is Tampa's. The rest of the city is still catching up.
