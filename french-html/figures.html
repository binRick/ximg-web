Key Figures
Maximilien Robespierre
The Incorruptible · 1758–1794
A provincial lawyer from Arras who became the most powerful man in France. Robespierre believed terror was the instrument of virtue — that the Republic could only survive by eliminating its enemies without mercy. His rigid idealism sent thousands to the guillotine, including former allies. Arrested on 9 Thermidor (July 27, 1794) and guillotined the next day.
Marie Antoinette
Queen of France · 1755–1793
Austrian princess who married the Dauphin at age fourteen. Caricatured as "Madame Deficit" for her extravagant spending at Versailles while France starved. Whether or not she actually said "Let them eat cake," the phrase captured public fury perfectly. Separated from her children in prison and guillotined on October 16, 1793, aged 37.
Napoleon Bonaparte
General · First Consul · Emperor
A Corsican artillery officer of minor nobility who rose through revolutionary merit rather than birth. His brilliant Italian campaigns in 1796–97 made him the most famous soldier in Europe. When the Directory collapsed in chaos, Napoleon seized power in the coup of 18 Brumaire (November 9, 1799), becoming First Consul — and effectively, dictator. Emperor by 1804.
Georges Danton
Voice of the Revolution · 1759–1794
A thunderous orator and lawyer who became Minister of Justice. His famous rallying cry — "Dare, dare again, always dare!" — helped save France from Prussian invasion in 1792. He founded the Committee of Public Safety, then tried to moderate the Terror when it turned on innocent people. Robespierre had him arrested and guillotined in April 1794.
Jean-Paul Marat
Friend of the People · 1743–1793
A radical journalist whose newspaper L'Ami du peuple (Friend of the People) stoked popular fury against aristocrats and moderates. He called for thousands of executions and was briefly imprisoned. His skin disease forced him to spend hours each day in a medicinal bath — where Charlotte Corday, a Girondin sympathizer, stabbed him to death on July 13, 1793. His death made him a revolutionary martyr.
Louis XVI
King of France · 1754–1793
A well-meaning but indecisive king who inherited an impossible fiscal crisis. He convened the Estates-General hoping to reform taxes; instead he unleashed a revolution. His flight to Varennes in June 1791 shattered the constitutional monarchy by revealing his desire to escape. After the discovery of his secret communications with Austria, the Republic tried him for treason and guillotined him on January 21, 1793.
