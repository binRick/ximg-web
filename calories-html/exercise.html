EPOC — Exercise Post-Oxygen Consumption

High-intensity exercise (HIIT, heavy lifting) creates an "afterburn effect" where elevated oxygen consumption continues for 12–48 hours post-workout. This increases total energy expenditure beyond what's burned during exercise itself. HIIT and resistance training generate more EPOC than steady-state cardio. However, the magnitude is often overstated — EPOC from a single HIIT session adds perhaps 50–100 extra calories.

NEAT — Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis

The calories burned from all movement that isn't formal exercise — fidgeting, standing, walking to the fridge, doing laundry. NEAT varies enormously between individuals (200–2,000+ kcal/day) and is a major driver of metabolic differences. A person with high NEAT (naturally active, fidgets, walks everywhere) can burn 500–1,000 more calories per day than a sedentary person of the same size. Getting a standing desk, parking farther away, or taking stairs makes a genuine difference over weeks and months.
