Lagers — Bottom-Fermented Beers

Czech Pilsner
ABV: 4–5% · Origin: Plzeň, Czech Republic
Pilsner Urquell invented the style in 1842. Soft water, Saaz hops, rich golden color, dense white head. Hoppy for a lager.
HoppyGolden
German Pilsner
ABV: 4.5–5% · Origin: Germany
Drier, crisper than Czech. More bitter. Bitburger, Beck's, Warsteiner. Uses harder water which accentuates bitterness.
CrispBitter
Munich Helles
ABV: 4.7–5.4% · Origin: Munich, Germany
Soft, malty, low bitterness. Created in 1894 by Spaten to compete with pilsner. The everyday beer of Bavaria.
MaltySoft
Märzen / Oktoberfest
ABV: 5–6% · Origin: Bavaria, Germany
Traditional Oktoberfest beer. Amber, toasty malt, moderate hops. Brewed in March, lagered through summer, released in fall.
ToastyAmber
Bock
ABV: 6–7% · Origin: Einbeck, Germany
Strong, malty lager. Dark amber color, minimal hops. Maibock (pale, spring), Doppelbock (very strong, 7–12%), Eisbock.
StrongMalty
Dunkel
ABV: 4.5–5.6% · Origin: Bavaria, Germany
Dark lager. Chocolate and bread malt character, low hops. The traditional Bavarian lager before pilsner took over.
DarkChocolaty
Schwarzbier
ABV: 4.4–5.4% · Origin: Thuringia, Germany
Black lager. Roasted malt but stays crisp and dry — not heavy. Less roasty than stout. The dark side of lager.
DarkCrisp
American Adjunct Lager
ABV: 4–5% · Origin: USA
Budweiser, Coors, Miller. Uses corn or rice alongside barley for a lighter body and clean finish. Globally the bestselling style.
LightCrisp
