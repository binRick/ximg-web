Warcraft: Orcs & Humans
1994 · MS-DOS / Mac
The game that launched the franchise. Humans defend Azeroth against an orcish invasion pouring through the Dark Portal. Blizzard's first major release, inspired by Dune II but set in a high-fantasy world. Simple resource gathering, base building, and small-squad combat laid the RTS foundation.
RTS
Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness
1995 · PC / Mac
A massive leap forward — naval combat, air units, and a full multiplayer suite via Battle.net's precursor. The Horde and Alliance clash across Lordaeron, Khaz Modan, and Quel'Thalas. The expansion, Beyond the Dark Portal, sent heroes back through to Draenor. Sold over two million copies.
RTS
Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos
2002 · PC / Mac
Introduced hero units with RPG leveling, creep camps, and the Night Elf and Undead Scourge as major factions. The story — Arthas's fall from paladin to death knight and the coming of the Burning Legion — is widely regarded as Blizzard's best-written campaign. The World Editor spawned thousands of custom maps including the original DotA.
RTS
Warcraft III: Reforged
2020 · PC / Mac
A remaster of Reign of Chaos and The Frozen Throne with updated 4K graphics and reworked cutscenes. The launch was controversially rough — promised cinematic cutscenes were missing, user-created maps lost their copyright protections, and the original game was replaced rather than offered alongside. Received the lowest Metacritic user score of any game.

World of Warcraft (Classic)
2004 · Windows / Mac
Launched November 23, 2004, WoW defined the MMORPG era. Players chose Alliance or Horde and explored Azeroth across Kalimdor and the Eastern Kingdoms. Forty-person raids like Molten Core, Blackwing Lair, and Naxxramas. Six original races, eight classes. WoW Classic relaunched the original content in 2019 to massive demand.
MMORPG
The Burning Crusade
2007 · Expansion 1
Opened the Dark Portal to Outland — the shattered remains of Draenor. Added two new races (Blood Elves for Horde, Draenei for Alliance), the flying mount system, and the Sunwell Plateau raid. Peak population: ~10 million. The expansion deepened Warcraft III lore, putting players face-to-face with Illidan Stormrage and the Burning Legion.
MMORPG
Wrath of the Lich King
2008 · Expansion 2
Widely considered WoW's peak expansion. Players journeyed to Northrend to confront Arthas, the Lich King, culminating in the Icecrown Citadel raid. Added the Death Knight hero class, achievement system, and Inscription profession. Reached the franchise peak of 12 million subscribers in 2010. The final confrontation with Arthas remains iconic storytelling.

Lore & Factions
⚔ Alliance
Humans · Dwarves · Gnomes · Night Elves · Draenei · Worgen · Void Elves · Lightforged · Kul Tiran · Mechagnomes · Earthen
The Alliance is a coalition of races united under ideals of justice, honor, and the Light. Founded after the Second War, it is anchored by the human Kingdom of Stormwind. The paladin orders, mage academies of Dalaran, and the Gnomeregan engineers all contribute to its strength. Its capital, Stormwind City, is the largest human city on Azeroth.
🔴 Horde
Orcs · Trolls · Tauren · Undead (Forsaken) · Blood Elves · Goblins · Highmountain · Nightborne · Mag'har · Zandalari · Vulpera · Earthen
The Horde began as an orcish war machine corrupted by demon blood — but was rebuilt by Thrall into a society founded on survival and honor. It shelters the displaced and outcast: the Forsaken undead, the blood-addicted Blood Elves, and jungle trolls. Orgrimmar, built in the harsh Durotar desert, is the Horde's beating heart.
The Cosmos
The Burning Legion
Primary Antagonists
A vast army of demons led by the fallen titan Sargeras. The Legion's goal is to unravel all life and magic in the universe. They have invaded Azeroth multiple times, drawn to the power of the world-soul sleeping within the planet. Their forces include eredar warlocks, pit lords, and the fel-corrupted orcish Horde of the First War.
The Titans
World-Shapers
Godlike beings of order who travel the cosmos shaping worlds and seeding them with life. They crafted the Pantheon — Aman'Thul, Eonar, Khaz'goroth, Norgannon, Golganneth, and Aggramar — before Sargeras betrayed them. The titan Azeroth herself sleeps within the planet, her awakening tied to the fate of all life on the surface.
The Old Gods
Void Entities
Ancient horrors imprisoned beneath Azeroth by the titans. C'Thun, Yogg-Saron, N'Zoth, and Y'Shaarj whisper madness to mortal minds. Their corruption seeps through Azeroth's crust, manifesting as the Emerald Nightmare in the Dream and driving servants to worship death and chaos. They cannot be slain without destroying the world-soul.
The Scourge
Undead Army
The undead army created by the Lich King, originally Ner'zhul's spirit bound to the Frozen Throne by Kil'jaeden. The plague of undeath swept through Lordaeron in Warcraft III, killing and reanimating millions including crown prince Arthas Menethil. The Scourge served as the Burning Legion's hammer until Arthas merged with the Lich King and broke free.
Iconic Characters
Arthas Menethil
The Lich King · Crown Prince of Lordaeron
Alliance → Scourge
The most iconic Warcraft character. Once a paladin devoted to protecting his people, Arthas was consumed by his obsession with destroying the undead plague at any cost. He took up the cursed runeblade Frostmourne, sacrificed his soul, slaughtered his own men at Stratholme, and eventually drove a sword through his own father's heart. He became the Lich King's champion and later merged with him entirely. Defeated in WoW: Wrath of the Lich King.
Thrall (Go'el)
Warchief · World Shaman
Horde
Son of Durotan, raised as a slave gladiator by the human lord Aedelas Blackmoore, Thrall escaped and united the scattered orcish clans into a new Horde built on shamanistic traditions rather than demon blood. He founded Orgrimmar and led the Horde through the Third War. Later became the Earth Warden, channeling the elemental powers of the world itself. One of gaming's most complex "villain-turned-hero" arcs.
Jaina Proudmoore
Archmage · Lady of Theramore
Alliance
One of the most powerful mages in Azeroth, Jaina was instrumental in forging the temporary Alliance-Horde truce during the Third War. She was a voice for peace for much of WoW's history — until the Horde bombed her city of Theramore with a mana bomb, killing thousands. Her hair turned white from the blast. The loss hardened her into an implacable foe of the Horde and later forced her to reckon with her own capacity for hatred.
Illidan Stormrage
The Betrayer · Lord of Outland
Illidari (Neutral)
Night Elf twin brother of Malfurion. Illidan absorbed the powers of the Skull of Gul'dan and transformed into a demon-human hybrid to fight the Burning Legion — at the cost of being imprisoned for 10,000 years by his own people. "You are not prepared" became one of gaming's most quoted lines. In WoW: Legion, his sacrifice proved essential to defeating Sargeras, reframing him as an anti-hero who paid a price no one else would.
Sylvanas Windrunner
Dark Lady · Banshee Queen
Horde → Maw
Former Ranger-General of Silvermoon, killed and raised as a banshee by Arthas during the fall of Quel'Thalas. She reclaimed her body, broke free of the Scourge, and founded the Forsaken — the free undead. As Warchief, she burned the Night Elf World Tree Teldrassil, killing thousands. In Shadowlands, it was revealed she had been manipulated by the Jailer for decades, adding tragic dimension to her villainy.
Anduin Lothar
Lion of Azeroth · Supreme Commander
Alliance
The greatest human warrior of the Second War, Lothar rallied the Alliance of Lordaeron after the fall of Stormwind. Descendant of Arathi kings and last of the Arathi bloodline, he was killed at Blackrock Mountain during the climactic battle of the Second War — betrayed by the orcish warlock Gul'dan's agents. He is honored by a statue at the Gates of Ironforge and is the namesake of Anduin Wrynn, WoW's boy-king.
Medivh
The Last Guardian · The Prophet
Neutral
Guardian of Tirisfal and the most powerful mage in Azeroth's history — possessed since birth by the spirit of the demon lord Sargeras. Medivh opened the Dark Portal that allowed the orcish Horde to invade Azeroth in the First War. He was killed by his own apprentice Khadgar and his childhood friend Anduin Lothar. Reborn as a spirit, he guided Jaina, Thrall, and Tyrande to Kalimdor to face the Legion in the Third War.
Gul'dan
First Warlock · Darkness Incarnate
Old Horde
The orcish shaman who made a pact with the Burning Legion, corrupting his entire race with demon blood and forging the first fel-warlock traditions. He orchestrated the creation of the Dark Portal, the sacrifice of the draenei, and the formation of the Shadow Council. Abandoned by the Legion when he opened the Tomb of Sargeras, he was torn apart by demons. His skull became a weapon of mass destruction used multiple times throughout the lore.
Franchise Timeline
1994
Warcraft: Orcs & Humans
Blizzard's first major title. Orcs from Draenor invade Azeroth through the Dark Portal. Humans and orcs fight for the kingdom of Stormwind.
1995
Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness
The Horde conquers much of Lordaeron. Naval and air combat added. Battle.net predecessor launched. Alliance ultimately prevails; Portal is destroyed.
1996
Beyond the Dark Portal (expansion)
Heroes pursue the orcish clans back to Draenor itself. Ner'zhul tears the world apart opening too many portals, creating Outland.
2002
Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos
Night Elves and Undead Scourge introduced. Arthas falls. Archimonde and the Burning Legion assault the World Tree. Sacrifice of Nordrassil stops the invasion. DotA born from the World Editor.
2003
The Frozen Throne (expansion)
Illidan pursues Arthas. Arthas merges with the Lich King. Blood Elves introduced. Naxxramas hinted. Kael'thas and Lady Vashj ally with Illidan in Outland.
2004
World of Warcraft launches
November 23. The MMO that changed the industry. Molten Core, Blackwing Lair, and Onyxia defined early raiding culture. Peak subscriber base begins climbing toward 12M.
2007
The Burning Crusade
Outland opens. 10 million subscribers. Players face Kael'thas, Vashj, and finally Illidan Stormrage in the Black Temple.
2008
Wrath of the Lich King
12 million subscribers — the all-time peak. Death Knight class. Icecrown Citadel. Arthas is finally defeated: "... I see only darkness before me." The expansion widely regarded as WoW's best.
2010–19
Cataclysm through Classic Re-release
Subscriber counts decline through expansions but game remains profitable. WoW Classic launches 2019 to unexpectedly huge demand, proving the original still resonates.
2020
Warcraft III: Reforged & Shadowlands
Reforged launches to the lowest Metacritic user score in history. Shadowlands breaks WoW pre-order records but disappoints on story delivery.
2022–24
Dragonflight & The War Within
Community reception improves. Dragonflight praised for quality-of-life overhaul. The War Within begins the "Worldsoul Saga" trilogy planned through three expansions.
