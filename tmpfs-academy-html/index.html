The Idea

Most "learn Linux" platforms test whether you can recall a fact. tmpfs-academy tests whether you can do the work. You go from "visit the site" to "working in a shell" in seconds, with nothing to install. Every challenge is solved by performing real work on a real system — and success is determined by inspecting the actual machine state (files, services, processes, network), not by self-reporting.

Each box is a single-tenant, ephemeral VM with no internet egress, so mistakes are expected and have no lasting consequences. Safe to break, objective to grade.

User Experience

Browse challenges. 52 challenges grouped Easy / Medium / Pro across sysadmin, deployment, and troubleshooting. No login needed to look around.
Sign in & start. Starting a session requires a Google sign-in popup; a fresh, isolated VM is then provisioned on demand, with a BIOS/boot animation during the wait.
Solve the task. The brief shows as a handwritten note and in the box's MOTD (e.g. "the web server is down — bring it back and make it survive a reboot"). Work it in the in-browser terminal.
Get graded. "Check" runs a validator against the live system and reports pass/fail; on success a teacher-marked "✓ COMPLETE" page shows. Tiered hints are available if you get stuck.
Session ends. The VM is destroyed on solve, End, browser close, idle, or hard cap — nothing to clean up. Progress, streaks, badges, and a skill rating are tracked on the leaderboard.

Cost Safety & Lifecycle

Every box is locked down and time-bounded: idle teardown after 5 min with no player input, a 15-minute hard cap, browser-close teardown, and server-side teardown on solve. An external reaper terminates any session-tagged instance older than 15 minutes, and per-session state is restart-safe so a control-plane restart can't orphan a running VM. Every session is logged append-only with a full terminal transcript, browsable in a password-gated admin view.
