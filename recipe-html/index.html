Recipes

Essential recipes every cook should know — organized by technique and occasion

Breakfast

Perfect Scrambled Eggs

Low and slow is the method. Whisk 3 eggs with a pinch of salt. Medium-low heat, add butter, pour in eggs. Using a rubber spatula, fold constantly — never stop moving them. Remove from heat while they look slightly underdone (carryover cooking finishes them). Add crème fraîche or butter off heat, season, serve immediately.

Key: Remove from heat 30 seconds early. The biggest mistake is high heat — it makes them rubbery.

French Omelette

3 eggs, salt, butter, chives. Beat eggs vigorously (no lumps). Hot non-stick pan, generous butter that foams. Pour eggs in, immediately start stirring rapidly with a fork while shaking the pan. When curds form but surface is still wet, stop stirring — let the bottom set 10 seconds. Roll one edge to center, fold over onto plate. The result: pale yellow, cigar-shaped, no color. This is technically difficult — practice.

Gordon Ramsay's method: the pan is "hot, hot, hot" then off, then on. Temperature control is everything.

Buttermilk Pancakes

1.5 cups flour, 2 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp baking powder, 0.5 tsp baking soda, 0.5 tsp salt, 1 cup buttermilk, 0.5 cup milk, 1 egg, 3 tbsp melted butter. Whisk dry ingredients separately from wet; combine minimally (lumps are fine). Rest batter 5–10 minutes — the baking soda activates and bubbles form, guaranteeing fluffy results. Cook at medium, flip once.

The acidity of buttermilk reacts with baking soda to create CO2 bubbles. Don't overmix — gluten development makes pancakes tough.

Homemade Granola

3 cups rolled oats, 1 cup nuts (almonds, pecans), 0.5 cup coconut, 0.5 tsp salt, 0.3 cup maple syrup or honey, 0.3 cup oil, 1 tsp vanilla. Toss well, spread thin on parchment-lined sheet. Bake 325°F for 25–30 min, stirring once at 15 minutes. Let cool completely on pan — it crisps as it cools. Add dried fruit after cooling.

Low temperature is key — too hot and the nuts burn before the oats toast. Spreading thin ensures even browning.

Overnight Oats

0.5 cup rolled oats, 0.5 cup milk (any), 0.5 cup yogurt, 1 tbsp chia seeds, 1 tsp maple syrup. Mix in a jar, refrigerate overnight (8+ hours). In the morning, top with fruit, nut butter, or granola. The oats hydrate completely by morning. Keeps 3–4 days refrigerated. Customizable infinitely — the base recipe is a canvas.

Use old-fashioned rolled oats, not quick oats (turn mushy) or steel-cut (won't soften enough).

Shakshuka

Eggs poached in spiced tomato sauce. Sauté onion, bell pepper in olive oil until soft. Add garlic, cumin, paprika, cayenne, cook 1 minute. Add crushed tomatoes, simmer 10 minutes. Make wells in sauce, crack eggs in. Cover and cook until whites set but yolks still runny (5–8 min). Finish with feta, fresh herbs, crusty bread for dipping.

The key is not overcooking the eggs — pull from heat when whites are just set. Residual heat will carry them over.

Full English Breakdown

Components and technique: Bacon: dry-fry back bacon in cast iron, fat side down first. Sausages: low heat, turn frequently to avoid bursting. Eggs: fried in butter, baste with hot fat. Beans: Heinz, never homemade. Tomatoes: halved, grilled or roasted. Mushrooms: whole portobello, butter, hot pan. Black pudding: sliced, fried briefly each side. Sequence matters — sausages take longest, start first.

Everything should be finished simultaneously and served on a warm plate. This is a logistical achievement.

Avocado Toast Variations

Base: good bread toasted, half avocado mashed with lemon, salt, pepper. Classic: flaky salt, red pepper flakes, olive oil drizzle. Upgraded: poached egg, everything bagel seasoning, micro greens. Mediterranean: cherry tomatoes, feta, kalamata olives, oregano. Japanese-inspired: sesame oil, soy sauce, nori, sesame seeds, cucumber. The quality of the bread and the ripeness of the avocado matter more than toppings.

Lunch

Perfect Grilled Cheese

The best is simple: two slices of soft white bread, American or sharp cheddar, salted butter applied to the outside. Medium-low heat in a heavy skillet. Cook covered for 2–3 minutes (the steam melts cheese), uncover, press gently, flip. The bread should be golden brown, the cheese fully melted but not oily. Mayonnaise instead of butter on the outside creates a better, more even browning.

Medium-low heat is crucial. High heat browns the bread before the cheese melts — the classic mistake.

BLT with Perfect Bacon

Bacon: lay strips on a rimmed baking sheet, oven at 400°F, 15–18 minutes. Oven bacon is perfectly flat, evenly cooked, and requires no attention. Toast good white bread, mayo generously on both slices. Stack: iceberg lettuce (crisp), 2 thick slices summer tomato (salted), 4–6 strips bacon. The bread-to-filling ratio matters — a BLT should be architecturally sound and should require both hands.

Oven bacon eliminates splattering and is far more consistent than stovetop. Don't skip salting the tomato — it draws out moisture and concentrates flavor.

Classic Caesar Salad

Traditional dressing (no anchovies? Not a Caesar): 2 anchovy fillets (mashed), 1 garlic clove, 1 egg yolk, 1 tsp Dijon, juice of half a lemon, 0.5 cup olive oil, 0.5 cup Parmesan, salt, pepper. Whisk egg yolk with garlic, anchovy, Dijon, lemon, then drizzle in oil slowly to emulsify. Toss with romaine leaves, croutons (torn day-old bread, olive oil, garlic, oven-toasted), more Parmesan.

The original Caesar dressing at Caesar's restaurant in Tijuana (1924) had no anchovy. Worcestershire sauce was the umami source. Both are traditional depending on who you ask.

Grain Bowl Formula

Template: Grain base (1 cup cooked: farro, quinoa, rice) + Roasted vegetable (2 components: squash, broccoli, or cauliflower, 425°F 25 min with olive oil) + Protein (chickpeas, chicken, soft-boiled egg, tofu) + Sauce (tahini-lemon, miso-ginger, harissa-yogurt, green goddess) + Garnish (fresh herbs, pickled onion, toasted seeds). Mix and match. Meal prep 5 bowls on Sunday by batch-cooking the grain and roasting a sheet of veg.

Vietnamese-Style Banh Mi

French baguette (crispy outside, airy inside), spread with mayo on both sides. Layer: Vietnamese cold cuts or char siu pork or tofu, then pickled daikon and carrot (julienned, quick-pickled in sugar-vinegar brine 30 min), fresh cucumber slices, jalapeño, cilantro. The contrast of warm/cold, crispy/soft, rich meat/bright pickles is the point. The baguette must be baked fresh — stale bread kills it.

Turkish Menemen

Scrambled eggs with tomatoes and peppers — Turkey's greatest breakfast and a perfect lunch. Olive oil, sliced green and red peppers softened 5 minutes. Add chopped tomatoes (or crushed canned), salt, pepper, pinch cumin, red pepper flakes. Cook down 10 minutes until jammy. Beat 3–4 eggs, pour in, stir slowly over medium heat until just set but still creamy. Eat from the pan with good bread. Add feta, sucuk sausage, or caramelized onions.

Dinner

Roast Chicken

Spatchcock method (faster, crispier): Remove backbone with scissors (save for stock), press flat. Season aggressively with salt 24 hours ahead (dry brine). Pat completely dry. 450°F on a cast iron, 35–40 minutes. Skin is shatteringly crispy, breast stays juicy because legs and breast finish together. Traditional method: 425°F whole bird, 60–75 minutes. Stuff cavity with lemon, garlic, herbs. Basting is unnecessary — opening the oven lowers temperature and slows cooking.

Dry brine 24 hours ahead: salt draws moisture out then back in, seasoning the meat through and drying the skin for maximum crispiness.

Beef Stew

Chuck roast, cut 2-inch cubes (don't go smaller — they'll fall apart). Pat very dry, season well, sear in small batches in hot Dutch oven — do not crowd (crowding steams instead of searing). The Maillard reaction on each cube builds flavor. Deglaze with red wine or beef stock, scraping browned bits (fond). Add broth, aromatics, bay leaves. Never boil — low simmer (325°F oven or stove-top lowest) for 2.5–3 hours. Potatoes and carrots added last 45 minutes.

The key words: dry meat, hot pan, small batches, low heat after braising begins.

Pasta Carbonara

4 egg yolks + 1 whole egg per 400g pasta. 150g guanciale (or pancetta), 80g Pecorino Romano + Parmesan, black pepper — that's it. No cream, ever. Render guanciale in its own fat. Cook spaghetti or rigatoni al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water. Off heat: toss pasta with guanciale fat, then add beaten egg-cheese mixture, tossing constantly, adding pasta water tablespoon by tablespoon to emulsify. The heat of the pasta, the starch in the water, and constant movement creates the creamy sauce.

The sauce must never cook above 160°F or the eggs scramble. Off heat, tossing vigorously, is how you control temperature.

Fish en Papillote

Cooking fish in a parchment paper parcel — the steam trapped inside gently poaches the fish in its own juices. Tear large parchment hearts, fold in half. On one half: layer of sliced fennel/zucchini, season, place salmon or halibut fillet, add cherry tomatoes, herbs, lemon slices, white wine splash, butter dot. Fold and crimp edges tightly. 400°F for 12–15 minutes. The parchment puffs dramatically in the oven. Open at the table for theatrical presentation.

En papillote is nearly impossible to overcook — the steam environment prevents the harsh dry heat that desiccates fish.

Stir-Fry Technique

The wok must be ripping hot — preheated empty 3–5 minutes over highest burner. Add high-smoke-point oil (peanut, avocado) — it should shimmer immediately. Protein first (in batches — do not crowd), sear and remove. Aromatics (garlic, ginger, scallion whites) — 20 seconds max. Vegetables in order of density. Return protein. Sauce goes in last: soy, oyster sauce, sesame oil, splash of Shaoxing wine. Wok hei (breath of the wok) is the smoky char that requires extremely high heat — difficult to replicate on a home range.

Cold protein, dry vegetables, hot wok. Wet vegetables or crowding drops pan temperature — you'll steam instead of stir-fry.

Risotto

Arborio or Carnaroli rice. Warm stock (6 cups) in a separate pot. Sauté shallots in butter, add rice, toast 2 minutes (grains turn slightly translucent). Add white wine, stir until absorbed. Begin adding hot stock one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Each addition absorbed before next is added. 18–20 minutes total. The constant stirring coaxes out starch — this creates the cream without any actual cream. Mantecatura: off heat, stir in cold butter and Parmesan vigorously until glossy and flowing.

Consistency should be "all'onda" — flowing like a wave when the pan is moved. If it stands up in a mound, it's too dry.

Sauces & Stocks

Chicken Stock

Start with cold water (not hot) — this keeps proteins from coagulating immediately, producing a clearer stock. Chicken carcasses/wings, onion (halved, charred cut-side down in dry pan), carrots, celery, peppercorns, bay leaves, thyme, no salt. Bring to simmer slowly — skim the gray foam for the first 30 minutes. Never boil — boiling emulsifies fat into the liquid, making it cloudy and greasy. Simmer 3–4 hours, strain through fine-mesh, cool, refrigerate. Skim solidified fat off top.

The cold water start and no-boil rule are the two most important techniques for producing a clear, clean stock.

Brown Butter (Beurre Noisette)

The simplest luxury. Melt unsalted butter in a light-colored pan (so you can see the color change) over medium heat. It will foam, then the foam will subside, and the milk solids will begin to toast. Swirl the pan. When the butter turns a deep amber and smells like hazelnuts and toffee — remove from heat immediately. Strain through fine mesh to stop cooking. Pour over fish, toss with pasta, drizzle on vegetables. One minute longer = burned butter.

A light-colored stainless pan lets you see the brown milk solids. Don't use nonstick — you won't see the color change in time.

Pan Sauce from Fond

After searing a steak or chicken breast, the brown crust stuck to the pan (fond) is pure flavor. Pour off excess fat, return pan to heat. Add shallot, cook 30 seconds. Deglaze with wine, brandy, or stock — scrape the fond up aggressively with a wooden spoon. The liquid dissolves the browned bits. Add more stock, reduce by half. Finish with cold butter cut into pieces, swirling the pan off heat to emulsify. Season, pour over protein. This takes 3 minutes and elevates any simple sear.

Hollandaise

The classic emulsified butter sauce for Eggs Benedict and asparagus. 3 egg yolks, 1 tbsp water, splash of lemon juice, whisked over a double boiler (bowl over simmering water — not touching) until thick and ribbon-like. Remove from heat. Slowly drizzle in 200g clarified butter while whisking constantly — the yolks emulsify the fat. Season with lemon, cayenne, salt. Temperature control is everything — too hot and eggs scramble, too cool and butter doesn't emulsify.

If it breaks (turns greasy), start with a new yolk in a clean bowl and whisk the broken sauce in as if it were butter.

Basic Vinaigrette

The 3:1 ratio: 3 parts oil to 1 part acid. 6 tbsp olive oil, 2 tbsp red wine vinegar (or lemon), 1 tsp Dijon mustard (emulsifier), pinch salt, pepper. Shake in a jar or whisk. The Dijon helps the oil and vinegar stay combined temporarily. Variations: add honey (honey-mustard), minced shallot (classic French), fresh herbs (fines herbes). Always dress salads at the last possible moment — acid wilts greens quickly. A properly dressed salad has a light, even coating on every leaf.

Beurre Blanc

Classic French white butter sauce. Reduce 0.5 cup white wine + 2 tbsp white wine vinegar + 1 minced shallot until almost syrup (1–2 tbsp liquid remains). Over low heat, whisk in 250g cold butter cut in cubes, adding gradually. The cold butter and constant motion create a stable emulsion. Strain out shallots. Season with salt, lemon. Serve immediately over fish, scallops, asparagus. Elegant, rich, surprisingly easy once you understand the emulsion.

Baked Goods

Banana Bread

The riper the bananas, the better — wait for fully black-spotted bananas (the starches convert to sugar, maximizing sweetness and moisture). 3 very ripe bananas mashed, 75g melted butter, 150g sugar, 1 egg, 1 tsp vanilla, 1 tsp baking soda, pinch salt, 190g flour. Mix wet then fold in dry minimally. 350°F, loaf pan, 55–65 minutes. The loaf is done when a skewer comes out with a few moist crumbs (not wet batter). Add walnuts, chocolate chips, or swirl in peanut butter.

Overmixing develops gluten and makes banana bread tough. Fold until just combined — lumps are acceptable.

Focaccia

500g strong white flour, 7g instant yeast, 10g salt, 400ml warm water, 50ml olive oil plus more. Mix until shaggy, let rest 30 min (autolyse). Perform 4 stretch-and-folds at 30-minute intervals. Oil a deep tray generously, transfer dough, cover and refrigerate overnight (cold fermentation develops flavor). Next day, dimple aggressively with fingers (the technique creating the wells that pool olive oil). Generous olive oil into every dimple. 220°C for 25 minutes. The top should be golden; the olive oil pools crisp.

Don't skip the overnight cold proof — it's where 80% of the flavor develops. The generous olive oil bath is not optional.

Browned Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Brown 225g butter first — this adds toffee and nutty notes impossible to achieve otherwise. Cool slightly, beat with 200g brown sugar + 100g white sugar until thick. Add 2 eggs + 1 yolk, vanilla. Fold in 360g flour, 1 tsp each baking soda and salt, 300g chocolate chunks. Refrigerate dough 24–72 hours (chilling allows flour to hydrate fully and sugar to caramelize better in oven). Bake 375°F, 11–12 min — pull when centers look underdone.

The extra yolk adds richness. Brown butter, extra yolk, and cold rest are the three upgrades that separate good from extraordinary.

Lemon Bars

Shortbread base: 225g butter, 100g powdered sugar, 280g flour, pinch salt. Press into 9×13 pan, bake 350°F 20 minutes until just golden. Curd: 300g sugar, 50g flour, 4 eggs, 2 yolks, zest of 3 lemons, 180ml fresh lemon juice. Whisk and pour over hot crust. Bake 325°F 30 minutes until curd is just set (slight jiggle in center). Cool completely before cutting — they must be chilled to slice cleanly. Dust with powdered sugar just before serving.

Tart Shell (Pâte Sablée)

200g cold butter, 100g powdered sugar, 1 egg yolk, 300g flour, pinch salt. Pulse in food processor until it resembles breadcrumbs, add yolk, pulse until dough just comes together. Rest refrigerated 1 hour. Roll 3mm thick, line tart tin, press into edges. Blind bake: prick base with fork, line with parchment and baking weights (or dried beans). Bake 350°F 15 minutes with weights, 5–8 minutes without until pale gold. Cool before filling. The blind bake prevents the dreaded "soggy bottom."

Cold butter is non-negotiable for a short, crumbly texture. Warm butter develops gluten and makes a tough, bread-like crust.

Quick Weeknight — 20 Minutes or Less

Pasta Aglio e Olio

The classic 6-ingredient Italian pantry pasta. 400g spaghetti. While it cooks: gently warm 8 sliced garlic cloves in 100ml olive oil — low heat, 5 minutes, until golden but not brown (burnt garlic is bitter). Add red pepper flakes. Reserve 1 cup starchy pasta water. Toss pasta with garlic oil, add pasta water tablespoon by tablespoon, tossing to emulsify into a glossy sauce. Parsley, Parmesan optional but not traditional. Simple perfection in 15 minutes.

Fried Rice

Day-old cold rice is non-negotiable — fresh rice is too moist and clumps. Wok or large non-stick, very hot. Scramble eggs first, set aside. High heat, add oil, cold rice — break up any clumps. Press rice against hot surface, let it sit 1 minute before stirring (creates the toasted bits). Soy sauce around the sides of the wok (not into the center — it hits hot metal and caramelizes). Return eggs, add frozen peas, scallions, sesame oil off heat.

The day-old rice rule: fresh rice steams in the wok. Cold leftover rice is dry and fries beautifully.

Sheet Pan Chicken Thighs

Bone-in skin-on thighs are forgiving and flavorful. Toss with olive oil, lemon zest, garlic, herbs, generous salt. Place skin-side up on a rimmed baking sheet with vegetables (potatoes, broccoli, cherry tomatoes) — everything in a single layer. 425°F for 35–40 minutes. The chicken fat bastes the vegetables. Skin should be golden and crispy. Bone-in thighs are almost impossible to overcook — the internal collagen keeps them moist to 185°F (unlike breasts).

Perfect Quesadilla

Flour tortilla, Oaxacan cheese or Monterey Jack (shred your own — pre-shredded has anti-caking powder that prevents proper melting). Dry skillet over medium — no oil on the pan. Cheese on half the tortilla, fold. Press flat with spatula. Cook 2 minutes per side until golden with dark spots. Rest 30 seconds before cutting — lets cheese set slightly. Serve with salsa, crema, and guacamole. The technique: dry pan, medium heat, patient browning.

Noodle Soup in 20 Minutes

Good stock is the prerequisite. Heat 1L chicken or miso broth. Add aromatics: ginger slices, garlic, splash of soy, sesame oil, chili oil. Add cooked noodles (rice noodles take 3 min, ramen from packet takes 2 min). Soft-boil an egg (7 minutes, ice bath, peel). Top with whatever's around: roasted leftover protein, wilted spinach, sliced scallion, sesame seeds, nori, crispy shallots from a jar. The broth does the work — a well-seasoned stock elevates any combination of toppings.

Grain + Roasted Vegetables

Fastest weeknight formula: cook a grain (quinoa 15 min, farro 30 min, instant rice 12 min). Cube any vegetable (squash, broccoli, cauliflower, zucchini, sweet potato), toss with olive oil, salt, spread single layer on sheet pan, 425°F 20–25 minutes. While those cook: make a quick sauce (tahini + lemon + water, or yogurt + harissa, or peanut-soy-ginger). Combine, add protein if desired, finish with fresh herbs. Endlessly variable, consistently satisfying.

Entertaining

Charcuterie Board

Components: 2–3 cured meats (prosciutto, salami, chorizo, coppa), 2–3 cheeses of different textures (aged cheddar, brie, manchego, blue), 2 spreads (honey, fig jam, grainy mustard), crackers + bread, pickles (cornichons, pickled peppers), fresh fruit (grapes, sliced pear), nuts (Marcona almonds, walnuts). Method: place large items first (cheeses, bowls of spreads), fill gaps with cured meats fanned or folded, then crackers, then fill every remaining space with fruit and nuts. Enough for 8: use a board at least 12×18 inches.

Beef Tenderloin (Reverse Sear)

The most impressive dinner party centerpiece. Season 2kg tied tenderloin aggressively with salt/pepper 24 hours ahead. Place on a wire rack over a sheet pan. 250°F oven until internal temp reaches 115°F (about 60–80 minutes). Remove, rest 10 minutes, then sear in ripping hot cast iron with butter, garlic, thyme — 1 minute per side, 4 sides. Rest 5 minutes. The reverse sear produces edge-to-edge perfect medium-rare with a dramatically better crust than forward-sear because the exterior is dry when it hits the pan.

Perfect Hummus

From scratch. 400g dried chickpeas soaked overnight, boiled until very soft (2+ hours — the secret is they must be mushy, not al dente). While warm: blend with 4 tbsp tahini, 2 lemons juiced, 2 garlic cloves, 1 tsp cumin, salt. Add ice water tablespoon by tablespoon while blending for 3–4 minutes until silky. The ice water and long blend time create the lighter-than-air texture. Spread on a wide plate, make a well, olive oil pool in center, paprika, chopped parsley, whole chickpeas.

Baba Ganoush

Char eggplants directly over a gas flame or under a broiler — the skin must be completely blackened and the interior collapsed and smoky. Let cool in a colander, peel carefully (the charred skin is the flavor). Scoop flesh, drain excess water, mix with 3 tbsp tahini, 2 tbsp lemon, 1 garlic clove (minced), salt. Do not over-mix — a slightly chunky texture is better. The smoky flavor is entirely from the charred skin — don't skip this step or you'll have mediocre eggplant dip.

Spanish Tortilla

Potato omelette — Spain's greatest contribution to bar food. 6 eggs, 3 medium potatoes (thinly sliced), 1 onion (thinly sliced), olive oil, salt. Gently confit potato and onion slices in generous olive oil (barely bubbling, 20 minutes) until tender but not brown. Drain, cool slightly. Beat eggs, season, fold in potatoes. In the same pan (some oil), pour egg-potato mixture. Cook low 5 minutes until edges set. The flip: place a plate on the pan, flip with confidence, slide uncooked side back in. Cook 3 more minutes. Serve at room temperature. The center should be slightly custardy.

Cheese Soufflé

Reputation far exceeds difficulty. Make a roux: 30g butter, 30g flour, cook 1 minute, add 250ml warm milk whisking constantly, cook until thick. Off heat, add 80g Gruyère, 3 egg yolks, seasoning. Beat 5 egg whites to stiff peaks (cream of tartar helps). Fold whites into base in three additions — gently, preserving volume. Buttered and Parmesan-dusted ramekins. Bake 375°F 12–14 minutes without opening the oven. Serve immediately — it falls after 2 minutes. It sounds dramatic but the technique is mechanical and reliable.

The only way a soufflé fails is if egg whites are under-beaten or the oven door is opened. Follow the steps and it will rise.
