Progressive Overload Principles

The fundamental law of strength training: consistently increase the demand on the muscle to force adaptation. Add weight (~2.5kg lower body, ~1.25kg upper) when you complete all target reps with good form.

Linear progression: add weight each sessionDouble progression: sets then weightPeriodization: wave loading

Recovery & Supercompensation

Supercompensation Curve

Training causes fatigue (performance temporarily decreases). During recovery, the body adapts above the original baseline — supercompensation. Train again at this peak to keep progressing.

Optimal window: 48–72hTrain too soon: overtrainTrain too late: lose adaptation

Recovery Pillars

  • Sleep — 7–9h, GH released in N3
  • Nutrition — protein synthesis window 24–48h
  • Stress — cortisol interferes with recovery
  • Deload — every 4–8 weeks, reduce volume 40%
  • Active recovery — light movement increases blood flow
