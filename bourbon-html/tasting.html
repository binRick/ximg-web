Sensory Appreciation

How to Taste Bourbon

Bourbon rewards patience and attention. A deliberate tasting reveals layers of complexity that a quick sip will miss entirely.

🥃

Neat

  • Use a Glencairn or copita glass — never a tumbler for serious tasting
  • Room temperature, no ice — cold suppresses volatile aroma compounds
  • Add one or two drops of still water to open up the nose dramatically
  • Rest the glass two minutes before nosing to let ethanol blow off
🧊

On the Rocks

  • Use a single large cube (2×2 inch) — it melts slowly, dilutes gently
  • Sphere ice is even slower — maximum chill, minimum dilution
  • Good for cask-strength bourbons that benefit from gradual opening
  • Less ideal for aged bottles where dilution mutes complexity
👁️

Look

  • Color depth — pale gold = young or wheated; deep amber = age or char
  • Legs — thick, slow-running legs indicate higher proof and oil content
  • Bead — shake gently; a rich bead of tiny bubbles indicates high quality
  • Clarity — non-chill filtered bourbons may go hazy when cold — that's good
👃

Nose

  • Keep lips slightly parted — this helps calibrate the ethanol
  • Smell from several inches away first, then gradually closer
  • Look for: vanilla, caramel, oak, dried fruit, baking spice, floral, grain
  • Retronasal: take a small sip, breathe out through the nose — new aromas emerge
👅

Palate

  • Entry — first impression, sweetness or heat, texture
  • Mid-palate — grain character, fruit, spice develop
  • Finish — what lingers and how long: short, medium, or epic
  • Chew the bourbon — roll it around, let saliva mix with it
📝

Finish & Score

  • Short — disappears in under 15 seconds (often young or light bourbons)
  • Medium — 15–45 seconds of pleasant warmth and flavor
  • Long — 60+ seconds, the mark of great aged bourbons
  • A warming, spiced finish = rye-forward; a sweet, creamy finish = wheated

From the Bar

Classic Bourbon Cocktails

🍊

Old Fashioned

Bourbon, sugar cube, Angostura bitters, orange peel. The original cocktail. Use a high-rye bourbon for a drier, more aromatic glass.

🍒

Manhattan

2 oz bourbon, 1 oz sweet vermouth, 2 dashes Angostura, cherry. Stirred, never shaken. A wheated bourbon softens beautifully with vermouth.

🌿

Mint Julep

Bourbon, fresh mint, sugar, crushed ice. The official drink of the Kentucky Derby since 1938. Over 120,000 served each Derby weekend.

🍋

Boulevardier

Bourbon, Campari, sweet vermouth. The Negroni's bourbon-drinking cousin. Richer, more amber, with that characteristic bittersweet finish.

✈️

Paper Plane

Equal parts bourbon, Aperol, Amaro Nonino, lemon juice. Modern classic by Sam Ross. Bright, tart, complex — best with a 100-proof bourbon.
