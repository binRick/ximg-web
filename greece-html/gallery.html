Gallery — Ancient Greece in Art & Architecture
The Parthenon, 447–432 BC
The crowning achievement of Athenian architecture, built under Pericles atop the Acropolis. Dedicated to Athena, goddess of wisdom, it remains the most perfect example of Doric architecture ever created.
The Acropolis of Athens
The sacred rock overlooking Athens has been continuously occupied since the Neolithic era. The Periclean building program of the 5th century BC transformed it into the architectural wonder seen today.
Alexander at the Battle of Issus
The famous Alexander Mosaic, discovered at Pompeii, depicts Alexander the Great charging toward the Persian King Darius III at the Battle of Issus in 333 BC — a decisive Macedonian victory.
Alexander the Great, 356–323 BC
A detail from the Alexander Mosaic showing his intense, determined expression in battle. By age 30, Alexander had created one of the largest empires in ancient history, from Greece to northwestern India.
Socrates, 470–399 BC
Roman copy of a Greek bust of Socrates. Described by contemporaries as physically unattractive with a snub nose, he compensated with formidable intellectual charisma. His execution by hemlock became a symbol of truth over tyranny.
Pericles, 495–429 BC
The statesman who led Athens during its Golden Age. Under his leadership, Athens built the Parthenon, expanded its empire, and became the cultural and intellectual center of the Greek world.
Plato's Academy
A mosaic from Pompeii (1st century BC) depicting Plato's Academy — the first institution of higher learning in the Western world, founded around 387 BC. Aristotle was its most famous student.
Greek Pottery — The Geometric Period
Ancient Greek ceramics were the canvases of their age. This vase by the Dipylon Painter dates from the 8th century BC, depicting funeral rites (prothesis) in the characteristic geometric style of the period.
