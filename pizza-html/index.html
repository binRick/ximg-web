Pizza

America's favourite slice — the biggest chains and the boldest styles, from coal-fired Neapolitan to Detroit's crispy-edged squares.

🗺️ US Regional Styles

New York Style
New York City, NY
Large, hand-tossed slices with crispy-chewy crust. Fold it lengthwise to eat. Sold by the slice at every street corner.
Chicago Deep Dish
Chicago, IL
Casserole-deep pan pizza with chunky tomato sauce on top of the cheese. Buttery crust, 30+ min bake time.
New Haven Apizza
New Haven, CT
Coal-fired, charred, oblong pies. White clam pizza is iconic. Chewier and saltier than NY style.
Detroit Style
Detroit, MI
Rectangular, baked in oiled steel pans. Cheese goes to the edge and caramelizes. Sauce ladled on top.
St. Louis Style
St. Louis, MO
Ultra-thin cracker crust cut into squares. Uses Provel cheese — a gooey processed blend unique to St. Louis.
California Style
Los Angeles, CA
Thin crust topped with fresh, non-traditional ingredients: avocado, smoked salmon, arugula. Gourmet-driven.
Tavern / Bar Style
Chicago & Midwest
Cracker-thin crust cut into party squares. Different from deep dish — the everyday Chicago pizza most locals eat.
New England / Greek
New England
Thick, oily pan crust. Oregano-forward tangy sauce. Brought by Greek immigrants. Dominant takeout style in CT, MA, RI.
Sicilian
New York / National
Square, thick, focaccia-like crust with a robust tomato sauce and stretchy mozzarella. Baked in a sheet pan.
Grandma Pizza
Long Island, NY
Home-style sheet-pan pizza. Olive oil fries the bottom. Dense, moist interior. Sauce goes over the cheese.
Colorado Mountain
Colorado
Thick honey-wheat crust with an earthy sweetness. Often topped with green chile, pinto beans, and jalapeños.
Boardwalk Pizza
Jersey Shore, NJ
Huge, extra-wide NY-style slices served at the shore. Distinctively light, foldable, eaten walking on the boardwalk.

🌍 Pizza Around the World

🇮🇹
Neapolitan
Naples, Italy
The original. Soft, charred, 90-second bake in a 900°F wood-fired oven. Protected by EU law (TSG).
🇮🇹
Pizza al Taglio
Rome, Italy
Rectangular pizza sold by weight, cut with scissors. Thick, airy Roman crust with creative toppings.
🇹🇷
Lahmacun
Turkey / Armenia
Paper-thin flatbread topped with spiced minced meat, tomatoes, and herbs. Rolled up and eaten with lemon.
🇫🇷
Flammekueche
Alsace, France
Tarte flambée — ultra-thin crust with crème fraîche, lardons, and onions. Baked in a wood-fired oven.
🇱🇧
Manakish
Lebanon / Levant
Flatbread topped with za'atar and olive oil, or cheese. A Levantine breakfast staple.
🇯🇵
Japanese Pizza
Japan
Often topped with mayo, corn, seafood, or teriyaki chicken. Thin crust, sweeter sauce, premium ingredients.
🇧🇷
Brazilian Pizza
São Paulo, Brazil
Thin crust, sweet sauce, and unconventional toppings like chocolate, banana, or guava with cheese for dessert.
🇦🇺
Australian Pizza
Australia
Often topped with egg (Aussie pizza), smoked salmon, or shrimp. Thin base, creative toppings influenced by Asia.
🇮🇳
Indian Style
India
Paneer tikka, tandoori chicken, and green chili on a thick crust. Spiced tomato sauce with masala seasoning.
🇷🇺
Russian Mockba
Russia
Topped with sardines, tuna, mackerel, salmon, and onion. A cold-serve pizza that's a genuine cultural quirk.
🇸🇪
Swedish Kebab Pizza
Sweden
Döner kebab meat, French fries, and kebab sauce on a pizza. Sweden's most popular takeout dish per capita.
🇵🇰
Karachi Pizza
Pakistan
Bold spices, seekh kabab toppings, green coriander chutney, and a thick South Asian-inflected crust.

🔥 Oven Guide

🪵
Wood-Fired
800–900°F · 60–90 sec
The gold standard. Extreme heat creates leopard-spotted char and perfectly puffed cornicione. Used for Neapolitan.
Use 00 flour dough, high hydration
Rotate every 20 seconds
Watch for hot spots near flame
⬛
Pizza Steel
500–550°F · 5–7 min
Best home method. Steel conducts heat faster than stone, producing crispy undercarriage close to wood-fired results.
Preheat steel 1 hour before baking
Finish 2 min under broiler
Use parchment for transfer
🪨
Pizza Stone
500°F · 8–10 min
Classic home method. Absorbs moisture from the dough base for a crisper crust. Cheaper than steel but slower to heat.
Preheat 45–60 min minimum
Never wet a hot stone — thermal shock cracks it
Cornmeal on the stone prevents sticking
🍳
Cast Iron Skillet
450°F oven + broiler · 12 min
Great for pan pizza and Detroit-style. Preheat on stovetop, add dough, then finish in oven for crispy, oily base.
Oil the pan generously
Press dough to edges before baking
Sauce goes on top of cheese
🔥
Ooni / Outdoor Pizza Oven
750–900°F · 60–90 sec
Propane or wood-pellet tabletop ovens that replicate Neapolitan heat at home. Best consumer product for authentic results.
Rotate with a turning peel every 20 sec
Let oven recover 2 min between pies
Works best with wet, extensible dough
🌀
Air Fryer
380°F · 6–8 min
Quick, surprisingly good for reheating and small personal pizzas. Crisps the bottom and melts cheese without drying out.
Don't crowd — one pizza at a time
No parchment — air must circulate
Great for reheating next-day slices
🔆
Regular Home Oven
450–500°F · 10–14 min
Most people's default. Results are good with the right setup — rack position and preheating time matter more than people think.
Rack on the bottom third of the oven
Preheat at least 30 min
Broil last 2 min for color
🥩
Charcoal Grill
500–600°F · 4–6 min
Direct-heat grilling gives the dough smoky char marks and rapid crust crisping. Flip-free with the lid closed.
Grill one side 2 min, flip, add toppings
Use a thin, stiff dough — less tear risk
Watch closely — it goes from perfect to burnt fast

🗳️ Hot Takes — vote on pizza controversies

997
First Written Record of "Pizza"
A Latin text from Gaeta, Italy records the word "pizza" as a local flatbread given as payment to a bishop. The proto-pizza was a simple focaccia-style bread, long before tomatoes reached Europe.
1522
Tomatoes Arrive in Europe
Spanish conquistadors bring tomatoes back from the Americas to Europe. Initially feared as poisonous, Neapolitan peasants eventually add the fruit to their flatbreads, creating the foundation of modern pizza.
1738
Antica Pizzeria Port'Alba — World's First Pizzeria
The world's oldest documented pizzeria opens in Naples, Italy, initially as a street stall for workers before moving to a permanent location. It still operates today on Via Port'Alba.
1889
The Margherita Is Born
Neapolitan pizzaiolo Raffaele Esposito creates the Margherita for Queen Margherita of Savoy, topping it with tomato (red), mozzarella (white), and basil (green) to represent the Italian flag. Pizza's defining recipe is set.
1905
Lombardi's Opens — First U.S. Pizzeria
Gennaro Lombardi opens America's first pizzeria at 53½ Spring Street in Manhattan, New York City. Italian immigrants flock to its coal-fired Neapolitan pies. Lombardi's still operates today and is a New York landmark.
1943
Chicago Deep Dish Invented
Ike Sewell and Ric Riccardo open Pizzeria Uno in Chicago, introducing the deep-dish style — a thick, buttery crust pressed into a pan with chunky tomato sauce layered on top of the cheese. A distinctly American pizza revolution begins.
1945
WWII Soldiers Bring Pizza Home
American GIs returning from Italy bring a taste for pizza with them. Demand for Italian-style pizzerias surges across the U.S., turning pizza from an ethnic niche food into a mainstream American staple.
1958
Pizza Hut Founded
Brothers Dan and Frank Carney open the first Pizza Hut in Wichita, Kansas with a $600 loan from their mother. Their pan pizza and red roof restaurants define casual family dining for decades. By the 1970s it becomes the world's largest pizza chain.
1960
Domino's Founded
Tom Monaghan and his brother buy a small pizza shop called DomiNick's in Ypsilanti, Michigan for $500. Tom later buys out his brother, renames it Domino's, and builds an empire on a revolutionary promise: 30-minute delivery.
1962
Hawaiian Pizza Is Invented
Sam Panopoulos, a Greek-Canadian restaurateur in Chatham, Ontario, adds canned pineapple to a pizza on a whim, creating the Hawaiian pizza. Polarizing to this day, it remains one of the most ordered toppings worldwide.
1984
Little Caesars "Pizza! Pizza!"
Little Caesars launches its iconic two-for-one "Pizza! Pizza!" campaign, making it the fastest-growing pizza chain in the world through the late 1980s and early 1990s. The Hot-N-Ready $5 pizza later becomes a cultural touchstone.
1994
First Internet Pizza Order
Pizza Hut takes what is widely considered the first commercial transaction on the internet — an online order for a pepperoni pizza placed in Santa Cruz, California. E-commerce begins with a slice.
2008
MOD Pizza — The Fast-Casual Revolution
Scott and Ally Svenson open the first MOD Pizza in Seattle, pioneering the fast-casual, build-your-own pizza model. The format — like Chipotle for pizza — explodes into a national trend, spawning dozens of competitors.
2010
Domino's Radical Reinvention
Domino's publicly admits its pizza "totally sucks" in a landmark ad campaign, rebuilds its recipe from scratch, and transforms its digital ordering. The move is a branding masterstroke — Domino's stock surges over 5,000% over the next decade.
2017
UNESCO Recognizes Neapolitan Pizza-Making
UNESCO inscribes the art of Neapolitan pizza-making ("pizzaiuolo") on the Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. The craft joins flamenco and the Mediterranean diet as a protected world tradition.
2020s
$46 Billion Industry
The U.S. pizza industry surpasses $46 billion in annual sales. Americans eat approximately 3 billion pizzas per year — roughly 23 pounds of pizza per person. Pizza is served in over 70,000 pizzerias across the country.
