Famous Ships
Queen Anne's Revenge
Frigate — Blackbeard's flagship
Originally a French slave ship (La Concorde). Blackbeard added 40 guns after capture in 1717. Ran aground off North Carolina in 1718. Wreck discovered in 1996.
Guns: 40
Crew: 300+
Tons: 200
Whydah Gally
Galley — "Black Sam" Bellamy
Captured by Bellamy from a slave-trading voyage. Carried £20,000 in gold and ivory. Sunk 1717 off Cape Cod. First authenticated pirate shipwreck excavated (1984).
Guns: 28
Crew: 150
Tons: 300
Royal Fortune
Warship — Bartholomew Roberts
Black Bart's final flagship — one of several ships he named Royal Fortune. Carried 40 guns. Defeated by HMS Swallow off West Africa in 1722, ending the Golden Age.
Guns: 40
Crew: 272
Tons: 400
Adventure Galley
Galley — Captain Kidd
Commissioned by William Kidd as a privateer. Sailed to the Indian Ocean, captured the Quedagh Merchant, and returned home — only for Kidd to be hanged for piracy in 1701.
Guns: 34
Crew: 150
Tons: 284
Fancy
Frigate — Henry Every
Every seized this 46-gun ship in a mutiny in 1694. The Fancy outran every navy vessel sent after it. Used to capture the Mughal treasure ship Ganj-i-Sawai in the most profitable pirate raid in history.
Guns: 46
Crew: 150
Tons: 500
Revenge
Sloop — Stede Bonnet
Purchased outright by the "Gentleman Pirate" Stede Bonnet — the only pirate known to have bought his own ship. A 10-gun Barbadian sloop. Bonnet was captured in 1718 and hanged at Charleston.
Guns: 10
Crew: 70
Tons: 60
Golden Hind
Galleon — Sir Francis Drake
Drake's ship on his 1577–1580 circumnavigation of the globe. Originally named Pelican. Raided Spanish treasure ports and captured the Nuestra Señora de la Concepción. Drake was knighted aboard it by Queen Elizabeth I.
Guns: 18
Crew: 85
Tons: 120
Ranger
Sloop — Calico Jack
John "Calico Jack" Rackham stole this sloop from Nassau Harbour in 1718, sailing off with his lover Anne Bonny. They cruised the Caribbean with a small crew until captured by a Jamaican pirate hunter in 1720.
Guns: 12
Crew: 40
Tons: 80
