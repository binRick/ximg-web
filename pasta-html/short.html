Short Pasta — Shapes designed to trap sauce

Short pasta shapes are engineered to hold chunky, hearty sauces in tubes, ridges, and cavities. The geometry is not decorative — each shape was designed to maximize the ratio of sauce to pasta in every bite.

Penne
Tubes, cut at angle
The hollow tube traps sauce inside while ridged exterior (rigate) holds sauce outside. All'arrabbiata or penne alla vodka are its classic partners. Penne rigate always preferred over penne lisce (smooth).
Rigatoni
Large ridged tubes
Larger than penne, cut straight. Deep ridges and the wide tube make it the best shape for absorbing chunky meat sauces. Rigatoni alla pajata (Roman veal intestine ragù) is its highest calling.
Fusilli
Corkscrew spirals
The spiral traps thick, chunky sauces beautifully — pesto clings to every groove, ragù wedges into every coil. Works warm or cold (pasta salad). Most versatile short pasta shape.
Farfalle
Bow ties / butterflies
Festive, visually charming. The pinched center stays slightly underdone while edges cook fully — two textures in one piece. Best with light cream sauces or pea-prosciutto combinations.
Orecchiette
Little ears (Puglia)
Thumb-pressed concave discs from Puglia. The cup shape holds chunky ingredients — the classic preparation is orecchiette with broccoli rabe and sausage. Chewy, hearty, made by hand traditionally.
Cavatappi
Double elbow spirals
Hollow, ridged spiral tube. Exceptional at holding thick cheese sauces inside and outside simultaneously. The best shape for mac and cheese according to many pasta enthusiasts.
Conchiglie
Shells
Shell-shaped pasta in various sizes (conchigliette to large conchiglioni). The shell holds chunky ingredients inside like a cup. Large versions can be stuffed and baked. Ricotta-stuffed conchiglioni is a southern Italian classic.
Paccheri
Very large smooth tubes
Enormous open tubes from Campania. Each pacchero is nearly a single bite. Best with luxurious seafood — fresh tuna, octopus, lobster — where the tube can be stuffed with a single piece.
Ziti
Straight long tubes
Long smooth tubes, often broken before cooking. The classic base for baked ziti — layered with ricotta, mozzarella, and tomato sauce. Common in Neapolitan-American cooking.
