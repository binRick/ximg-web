Stuffed & Filled Pasta

Stuffed pasta requires fresh egg dough thin enough to reveal the filling through the translucent skin. Each region of Italy claims a different filled pasta as its own — a matter of fierce local pride.

Ravioli
Square-cut pillows
The most universal stuffed pasta. Square or rectangular pockets of egg pasta filled with ricotta, spinach, meat, or various combinations. Served with butter-sage, cream, or light tomato. Endless regional variations.
Tortellini
Ring-shaped (Bologna/Modena)
Small rings of pasta filled with a mix of pork, prosciutto, mortadella, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and nutmeg. The filling recipe was registered with the Bologna Chamber of Commerce in 1974. Served in broth (in brodo) is the traditional and correct way.
Tortelloni
Large tortellini
Larger version of tortellini, typically filled with ricotta and spinach (or butternut squash). Less intricate than tortellini; more filling per bite. Served with butter and sage or walnut sauce.
Gnocchi
Potato dumplings
Not technically pasta — potato-based dumplings that cook quickly and float when done. Incredibly light when done well, dense and leaden when wrong. Thursday is gnocchi day in Rome (Gnocchi di giovedì).
Cannelloni
Rolled tubes, baked
Large fresh pasta sheets rolled around filling (usually ricotta-spinach or meat ragù), placed in baking dish, covered in béchamel and tomato sauce, baked. A labor-intensive Sunday dish.
Agnolotti
Piedmontese pillows
Piedmont's stuffed pasta — small square pillows filled with braised meat and vegetables. The filling is made from the leftover braised meats of the previous night's dinner. Served in the braising juices or with butter and fresh truffles.
